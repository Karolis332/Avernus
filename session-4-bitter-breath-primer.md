# Session 4 Primer — The Palace of Gore: Bitter Breath

## DESIGN THESIS

> Bloodbath was a wall to break. Bitter Breath is a riddle to solve under fire.

This fight is **not** harder because numbers go up. It is harder because:

1. **The Moonkite is on a clock that started before initiative was rolled.** Damage to Bitter Breath does not stop the clock. Only specific, costly actions do.
2. **Every "obvious correct play" has a hidden cost.** Sealing the door also seals their retreat. Destroying chains fast hurts the Moonkite. Killing Thakk first frees the Consort to nuke. The optimal route exists but it is not visible — they have to *read* the room.
3. **Bitter Breath learns.** Repeated tactics stop working. He banks Legendary Resistance for the spells that matter. He targets concentration on whoever just saved a prisoner.
4. **Three forced ethical choices** before the killing blow: the hostages, the prisoners, and — at the end — what Aurora is willing to lose.

**Win condition is not "Bitter Breath at 0 HP."** Win condition is *"Bitter Breath dead AND Moonkite alive AND the party can live with what they did."* Communicate this to the players the moment they enter the throne room. Show them the clock. Put tokens on the table.

## RECAP — WHERE WE LEFT OFF

- The party rode the **Lady Vengeance** down the river of blood, **casting *Air Bubble*** to seal the crew against the Choking Pall.
- They **fought through 5 chain devils** in the lower drainage tunnels (Bitter Breath's new infernal allies — replacing his brother's maw demons).
- They **fired the ship's arcane cannons to collapse a section of tunnel**, blasting open the route forward.
- The Lady Vengeance is now **wedged behind the collapse** — too large to follow, the crew sealed off behind tons of fused bone and blackite. **There is no ship-borne extract. They walk out, or they don't walk out.**
- They are now standing in the cooling silence of the Palace's lower passages, bone dust still drifting in the air, the Throne Room entrance ahead.

**Cold open line:**

> The dust settles. Behind you, the tunnel is choked with rubble — the Lady Vengeance's last cannonade did its work. Ahead, the passage opens into a vaulted chamber of fused bone and blackite stone. The ceiling is thirty feet high, ribbed with what look like the spines of enormous creatures. Gore drips from the joints. Not leaking. *Pulsing.* Like the building has a heartbeat.
>
> And somewhere deep below, threaded through the silence — *a sound.* Not wind. Not water. A song made of grief, almost too quiet to hear. Aurora — your wand is *humming* in tune with it. The Moonkite is alive. Barely.

| **End of every 2nd round in Phase 2** | -1 Vitality |

## PRE-SHOW: THE APPROACH (20 min)

### The Walk-Up

Two short beats before initiative:

**Beat 1 — The Smell.** As they cross the threshold into the upper Palace, the air changes. The smog they fought against is *gone*. Inside the Palace, the air is cold, dry, electric. Wrong-clean. Like a slaughterhouse that's been bleached.

**Beat 2 — The Voice (Aurora only — pass her a note).**

> *"You are close. He has been cutting me. Quickly.*
> *I can feel three of you. The cleric burns with a god he does not love. The hunter carries a lamp that could eat me too. And you. You I have known since before you had a name.*
> *Do not be a hero. Be a sister."*

The Moonkite is not asking to be saved. It is asking Aurora to make a *specific* hard choice later. Plant the seed.

### The Throne Room Reveal

> The passage opens. Six hobgoblins in crude plate stand in formation before a throne of fused weapons and shattered shields. To the left: a wall of scarred muscle in spiked plate, gripping a tower shield bolted to the arm — **Gorewarden Thakk.** To the right, hanging from the ceiling by its own spines, a barbed devil watches with cold calculation — **the Barbed Consort.**
>
> And on the throne: smaller than you expected. Lean. Coiled. Horns swept back like blades. Not lounging — *perched.* He turns a curved gutting blade slowly in one hand. His eyes are yellow.
>
> *"You smell like my brother's blood."*
>
> *"Sit down. I want to hear how he screamed."*

### THE HOSTAGES (NEW — Critical Setup)

Behind the throne, **four iron cages** hang from chains. Inside each: a prisoner. Bitter Breath has been collecting leverage since he took the Palace.

| Cage | Prisoner | Hook |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A **Hellrider sergeant** in tattered red — survived an ambush. Looks at Drenwal with recognition. *"…you wear the colors. Brother. Don't let me die here."* |
| 2 | A **young tiefling smuggler** — Smiler's lieutenant, captured during a raid. Knows Smiler's whereabouts. |
| 3 | A **half-mad githzerai monk** — fell through a planar tear. Mutters about a "river of mirrors." Future plot hook. |
| 4 | A **mortal infant**, swaddled, asleep, unaware. No explanation. Bitter Breath's pet horror. |

**Bitter Breath's Rules of Engagement (read aloud after the throne speech):**

> *"You came to my house. So we will play house rules.*
> *Every two rounds you do not bring a weapon to my throat — I cut a cage's chain. The cage drops. The floor here is forty feet down, into the blood pool. They won't survive.*
> *You can save them. I encourage it. Saving them is not killing me. The clock keeps ticking either way."*

**Mechanic — The Cage Cut:** At the end of every **even** round (round 2, 4, 6…) of Phase 1, Bitter Breath uses one legendary action to **cut a cage chain.** The cage falls. Prisoner inside dies in the blood pool unless saved that round (see below).

**Saving a prisoner:**
- **DC 14 Athletics** (running grab) or **DC 13 Acrobatics** (catching the chain) — uses an action
- **Misty Step / Levitate / Feather Fall / Mage Hand (with strength)** — costs a spell slot
- A PC who saves a prisoner takes **one Gutripper Strike legendary action immediately** (Bitter Breath punishes the rescue) — +13 to hit, 2d8+7 slashing + 2d6 necrotic. He WILL connect, and he will explain it: *"That was charity. This is the bill."*

**Why this matters:** Each rescue costs a turn AND ~30 HP. The party WILL feel the trade. The infant cage is the cruelest — most parties will burn resources on it. **Let them.** Then make them feel the weight when Bitter Breath cuts the next one.

**Optional escalation:** If the party ignores the hostages entirely (just zerg-rushes Bitter Breath), reward that decision narratively but mechanically: each dropped prisoner gives Bitter Breath +1 to all saves for one round (he drinks the death). Three drops = he's almost untouchable for a moment. Show the cost.

### Enemies

**Bitter Breath** — AC 19, HP 280, Speed 50 ft. Full stat block in `bitter-breath-statblock.md`. **Treat "stunned" on Rotten Breath as DAZED instead** (target picks one of action/bonus action/movement). Don't delete a player's turn.

**Gorewarden Thakk** — AC 20, HP 90. Tower shield bolted to his arm. **Shield Wall**: while within 5 ft of Bitter Breath, attacks against BB have disadvantage; can use reaction to redirect a hit. **Tactical Shove** (bonus): DC 14 Str or pushed 10 ft, prone — uses this to push PCs into blood pools or away from prisoners. **Multiattack**: 2 cleavers, +7, 1d10+4. **Death trigger:** mooks make DC 12 WIS or scatter (frightened 1 round).

**The Barbed Consort** — AC 15 (effective AC 20 on ceiling, three-quarters cover). HP 110. Standard barbed devil + **Counterspell 1/day** (saves it for Spirit Guardians or Banishment — telegraph the eyes-flash). **Hurl Flame** prioritizes Aurora (concentration breaker), then Drenwal. **Death trigger:** falls into nearest blood pool, sprays acidic blood (10 ft, DC 13 Dex, 2d6 acid).

**Hobgoblin Mooks (1-HP minions)** — AC 18, 1 HP. +5 to hit, **flat 8 damage** (no roll). Martial Advantage: **+7 flat** if ally adjacent. Reinforcements: **1d4 spawn from barracks door at initiative 20 every 2 rounds. Cap at 8.**

### Secondary Objectives — THE TRAPS

These look like good ideas. Each has a hidden cost. Players don't get to know the cost in advance.

| Objective | Effect | Hidden Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Destroy the Gore Throne** (AC 15, 40 HP, immune poison/necrotic) | Aura of Rot shrinks 30 ft → 15 ft | **Bloodbath's Echo lair action becomes free / passive** — every round, a ghostly silhouette of Bloodbath flickers over the wreckage. PCs within 30 ft must DC 13 WIS or take 1d8 psychic (his memories cling). |
| **Seal the Barracks Door** (DC 17 Athletics, DC 16 Thieves' Tools, or 30 damage) | No more mook reinforcements | **Their retreat is also sealed.** Phase 2 gets harder — no fleeing back up. AND: a chain devil reinforcement (the 6th, sent by Bitter Breath via telepathy) was *behind* that door. If sealed, it claws through after Phase 1 ends, joining Phase 2 fresh. |
| **Kill Thakk first** | Shield Wall ends, mooks scatter | The Consort is now free to focus 100% on the casters. Hurl Flame every round on Aurora. |
| **Kill the Consort first** | No Counterspell threat, casters breathe | Bitter Breath stops getting hit by the Consort's stray Hellfire. He gets +1 to all attacks (no longer dodging friendly fire). And Thakk is unkilled — Shield Wall stays up longer. |
| **Save all 4 hostages** | Moral high, future allies (Hellrider sergeant becomes a recurring NPC) | Burned ~4 actions and 100+ HP across the party. Phase 2 starts with the party near-broken. |

**Communicate the consequences AFTER they happen, not before.** Let them learn the cost. *"You realize, as the throne crumbles, that something stays — a presence, watching. Bloodbath's echo, denied a tomb."*

### Lair Actions: Phase 1 (init 20, no repeat)

1. **Gore Geyser.** DC 16 Dex, 3d8 necrotic + prone. 10 ft difficult terrain.
2. **Bone Cage.** DC 16 Str or restrained. Cage AC 12 / HP 25.
3. **Bloodbath's Echo.** 30 ft of throne (15 ft if destroyed): DC 15 WIS or frightened of throne for 1 round.
4. **Cage Cut (every 2nd round, mandatory).** Severs one hostage chain. Replaces the chosen lair action that round.

### Bitter Breath's CUNNING — He Reads The Party

Run him as a **chess player who hates losing.** Apply these adaptive rules:

- **Rule of Repetition.** The second time the party uses the same tactic (e.g., Aurora casts Eldritch Blast at full power twice in a row, or Asimov flanks via the same pillar), Bitter Breath responds. *"You did that already."* Examples: he Repositions to break flanking, he uses Bone Cage on Asimov, he tells the Consort to Counterspell *whatever spell Aurora just cast successfully* (he's learned it's important).
- **Concentration Hunter.** Whenever a PC saves a prisoner OR makes a Religion/Arcana check, Bitter Breath uses a legendary Gutripper Strike on them next opportunity. *"You looked away. Big mistake."*
- **Legendary Resistance Banking.** Track 3 LR tokens visibly on the table. Burn them ONLY for: Banishment, Hold Monster, Hypnotic Pattern, or any save-or-die. Make players see him save those. When the third token comes off, narrate: *"Something cracks behind his eyes. He shakes off your spell — but barely. You saw the fear."* Now they know the next save lands.
- **Backline Targeting.** If Asimov sneak attacks turn 1, BB uses Skirmisher to dart 50 ft to the casters and Multiattacks Aurora. Don't always go for the easy melee target.
- **The Banter.** He doesn't trash-talk. He *diagnoses.*
  - To Drenwal: *"Your god isn't in this room. Did you notice? He let you walk in here alone."*
  - To Aurora: *"That song you hear? I taught it to scream."*
  - To Asimov: *"You smell like soul-rot. The lamp on your hip is louder than you are."*

### Phase 1 Tactical Script

**Round 1.** BB opens with **Rotten Breath** through the cluster (40 ft cone, DC 17 Con, 8d8 poison + 4d6 necrotic, dazed on fail-by-5). Thakk shoves the closest melee toward a blood pool. Consort opens Hurl Flame on Aurora (4d6 fire, Concentration check). Mooks form a screen between PCs and throne. Lair: Gore Geyser under whoever pushed forward fastest.

**Round 2.** **CAGE CUT 1.** Bitter Breath gestures lazily at Cage 1 (the Hellrider). The chain parts. Read: *"Tick."* Then he uses Multiattack on whoever is wounded (Blood Frenzy advantage). Skirmisher behind Thakk after.

**Round 3.** Consort holds Counterspell for the first big spell — Spirit Guardians, Banishment, anything that will *change* the fight. Mooks reinforce from barracks door (1d4). Lair: Bone Cage on whoever made progress on a secondary objective.

**Round 4.** **CAGE CUT 2.** Bitter Breath cuts Cage 4 (the infant) — *because he knows it will hurt them most.* Don't editorialize. Just describe the chain parting and the basket falling. Let the table go silent. *Then* roll for whoever's saving it.

**Round 5+.** Adapt. If party is rolling well, escalate (Phalanx Command, Flaying Rush). If party is hurting, ease back — let Thakk fall to a critical, give them the catharsis.

### Phase 1 Transition (BB at 140 HP)

When Bitter Breath crosses 140 HP (Bloodied), he doesn't die. He **bolts.**

> Bitter Breath staggers. Blood — his own — drips from a wound on his flank. He touches it. Looks at his fingers.
>
> His expression doesn't shift to fear. It shifts to something worse.
>
> *"You want to take everything from me. My brother. My throne. My fortress."*
>
> He grabs the armrest of the gore throne and RIPS it free — a jagged slab of fused weapons and bone. He swings it into the nearest pillar. The pillar CRACKS. Dust rains from the ceiling.
>
> *"Then come TAKE it."*
>
> He turns and BOLTS — not through a door — through the FLOOR. His horns punch through stone behind the throne, opening a ragged hole. You hear him crashing down. Toward the prison. Toward the Moonkite.
>
> The room shudders. From below, a pulse of silver light. Then a scream that isn't sound — it's inside your heads. The Moonkite knows what's coming.

**Transition mechanics:**
- BB drops to the Cage Chamber (one level below).
- Surviving mooks/Consort fight 1 more round, then flee or surrender (choose your moment for catharsis).
- Party can drop through the hole (DC 12 Acrobatics, 1d6 bludgeoning) or take the spiral stair (slower, no damage).
- **Moonkite Vitality check.** Tally losses so far. Show the table.

## INTERMISSION — THE DESCENT (25 min)

This is where the choices crystallize. The party is bruised. They have decisions. Don't rush.

### CHOICE 1 — Rest or Push

**Rest (10–60 min):** Warlock slots back. Hit dice. Spell management.
- **Cost:** 1 Vitality per 10 min. Bitter Breath connects fully to chains (Phase 2 Siphon Regen at full 10 HP/chain/round).
- **Benefit:** Moonkite's residual aura grants +1 hit die per PC during rest (the only nice thing in Avernus).

**Push (immediate):** Skip the rest entirely.
- **Cost:** Nothing recovered. PCs go in wounded.
- **Benefit:** Bitter Breath hasn't fully siphoned — Phase 2 Siphon Regen reduced to 5 HP/chain/round. Vitality clock paused during transition.

**There is no right answer.** Let them argue.

### CHOICE 2 — The Prisoners

The hostages they saved are now **with them in the spiral passage.** They cannot fight. They cannot defend themselves. They will slow the party.

**Bring them down to Phase 2:**
- They are present in the cage chamber. Bitter Breath WILL target them with hostage strikes (see Phase 2). Each one in-room is a soft target.
- **If the Hellrider sergeant survives Phase 2/3:** he becomes a recurring ally — Drenwal gains a Hellrider contact who can host a future "rebirth" ritual. Significant arc payoff.

**Leave them in the upper passage:**
- They survive the Palace collapse only if the party returns within 10 min after the kill. Otherwise dead.
- Drenwal's god (whichever god he's leaning toward) notices the abandonment. -1 to all Religion checks for 1 session.

### CHOICE 3 — Asimov's Bounty Offer (private to Asimov — pass a note)

> The Soul Capacitor hums. Warm. The crystalline channels pulse purple. They're sensing something below. A soul. A *strong* soul. Rasheem's voice, distant, half-amused:
>
> *"That's a medium-grade soul down there, Asimov. Maybe leaning heavy. Kill it clean and the lamp drinks deep.*
> *But here's the thing. The lamp's also picking up something *else* down there. Something celestial. The Capacitor wants it. Bad. If you let it drink the celestial light first — the moonkite-thing — you'll get a payday like nothing the syndicate's ever seen. We're talking *retire-young* money.*
> *Of course. The bird-deer dies. But who's it really to you?"*

**Don't telegraph this to the table.** Asimov knows. He can act on it in Phase 3 if he wants. The Soul Capacitor has a *betrayal option.*

### CHOICE 4 — Drenwal's Vision (private to Drenwal — pass a note)

> A jolt behind your eyes. Dumal — not far. Standing on a ridge, looking south. He felt the Moonkite's pulse. He's coming. Twelve, maybe fifteen hours.
>
> Then Gargauth, oily and intimate:
>
> *"The cure, Drenwal. 'Die and be reborn in the eyes of another god.' What you don't realize is — you can choose. Right here. Right now. The Moonkite is a celestial. If you offer yourself to it as it dies — give it your divinity, your blood, your name — it might catch you. You might be reborn under a kinder god than mine.*
> *Or you might not. The Moonkite might be too weak to catch a falling soul. Then I catch you. And we both know what happens then.*
> *But it's a chance. Isn't it?"*
>
> **Bhaal Influence: tick to 4/6.**

**Drenwal's option in Phase 3:** spend his action AND his Channel Divinity AND drop to 0 HP voluntarily to attempt the cure. This triggers a divine intervention table (DM call — three results: Moonkite catches him / a third god catches him / Bhaal catches him). High risk, high reward, permanent character change either way.

### The Scene

> You drop into the chamber and the temperature plummets. The air is cleaner here — cold, almost sharp — but threaded with something electric. Wrong.
>
> The cage hangs in the center. A 10-foot sphere of black metal, suspended by four chains that run into the walls, floor, and ceiling. Each chain pulses with sick orange light. Inside the cage: silver-white, folded, fading. The Moonkite. Smaller than it should be.
>
> Bitter Breath is wrapped around two of the chains. His claws dig into the metal. Orange light crawls up his arms and into his horns, which are now faintly glowing. His wounds from above are closing. Slowly. Visibly.
>
> He's feeding on the Moonkite.
>
> He turns his head. One eye is still yellow. The other has a ring of silver in it, stolen starlight leaking into his iris.
>
> *"You're too slow."*
>
> He releases one chain and drops to the floor. His aura pulses. The air around him shimmers with heat-haze, but the haze is tinged silver.
>
> *"Every second you wasted up there, I was down here. Drinking."*
>
> *"Do you know what celestial light tastes like?"*
>
> *"It tastes like winning."*

### Bitter Breath: Siphon State

**HP:** 140 (or +20 per 10 min the party rested in intermission, max 200). All other stats from base block.

**New trait — Siphon Regeneration:** At the start of each of his turns, Bitter Breath regains **HP equal to 10 × intact chains** (40 max). This is the secondary objective. Make it visible — *"He regains 30 hit points. The chains pulse."*

**New bonus action — Chain Leash (replaces Skirmisher):** Bitter Breath grabs a chain and swings to any point within **20 ft of an intact chain.** Insane mobility. He uses this to:
- Put himself between the party and the chain they're trying to break
- Bodyblock the rogue trying to lockpick
- Cross the room to interrupt Aurora's stabilize

**New action — Hostage Strike (3/Phase 2, replaces 1 of his 3 multiattacks):** Bitter Breath **rips a thread of stolen Moonkite essence from a chain and hurls it at a target.** Ranged spell attack, +9 to hit, 30 ft range, 4d8 radiant damage. **Each use costs the Moonkite 1 Vitality.** He grins when he uses it: *"It hurts more when it comes from her."*

**Corrupted Aura:** Aura of Rot now adds **1d6 necrotic** to the failed save effect. Drenwal will feel this most.

### THE CHAIN PUZZLE — The Real Phase 2 Challenge

Each chain has **AC 14, 25 HP, immune to poison and necrotic.** Vulnerable to radiant.

**Three ways to remove a chain — each with consequences:**

| Method | Action Cost | Vitality Cost | Bonus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Damage (fast)** | Standard attacks/spells until 0 HP | **-1 Vitality per chain** (the chain backlashes into the Moonkite when destroyed violently) | Fast. Anyone can do it. |
| **Religion purify (Drenwal)** | Action while touching chain. DC 15 Religion. | 0 (safe) | On success: chain shatters in pure light. All allies within 30 ft regain 1d8 HP. **On natural 1–5 (Bhaal interferes): the channel turns necrotic. Chain still breaks but Moonkite loses 2 Vitality and Drenwal takes 2d6 necrotic.** |
| **Arcana resonance (Aurora, via wand)** | Action. DC 14 Arcana. | 0 (safe) | On success: chain dissolves into starlight. Aurora regains 1 spell slot of 1st level. **On natural 1: the wand backfires; Aurora takes 2d8 force and the wand goes dim for 1 round (no spellcasting focus).** |
| **Thieves' Tools (Asimov)** | Action while adjacent to chain anchor. DC 17 Thieves' Tools. | 0 (safe) | On success: surgical disconnect. No HP, no light, no spectacle. The chain just goes slack. **On failure by 5+: Asimov's tools snag — chain whips and deals 2d6 bludgeoning + restrained until end of his next turn.** |

**The Trap:** The party will instinctively damage the chains (it's the most familiar tool). They lose 4 Vitality if they do all four that way. **Let them learn this on chain 1 — describe the Moonkite cry out when the chain shatters violently.** Then they'll know. Then they have to choose between fast-but-painful and slow-but-safe.

### Lair Actions: Phase 2 (init 20, no repeat)

1. **Chain Lash.** One intact chain whips at a target within 15 ft. +10 to hit, 3d6 bludgeoning + 2d6 necrotic. Pulls 10 ft toward cage.
2. **Silver Scream.** All within 30 ft: DC 14 WIS or 2d8 psychic + dazed. Aurora has advantage.
3. **Gore Eruption.** Active blood font overflows. 15-ft-radius: DC 14 Str or prone + difficult terrain 1 round.
4. **Cage Pulse (only at 2+ chains broken).** All within 20 ft: DC 15 Con or 3d6 radiant. **Bitter Breath takes this damage too** — the stolen energy is becoming unstable.

### Bitter Breath's Cunning — Phase 2 Adaptive Behavior

- **He protects the chains.** If a PC engages a chain, he Chain-Leashes adjacent and Multiattacks them. He will NOT use Rotten Breath if it would clip a chain.
- **He uses Hostage Strike when desperate.** Not opener — saves it for when 2 chains are down, to make the Vitality clock catastrophic.
- **He targets purifiers specifically.** If Drenwal touches a chain to purify, BB uses legendary Gutripper Strike on him to break concentration / interrupt the check.
- **He talks while he fights:**
  - 1 chain down: *"That changes nothing."*
  - 2 chains down: *"You don't understand what you're doing. This power was WASTED on that thing. I'm USING it."*
  - 3 chains down: *"ENOUGH. If I can't keep it, neither can you."* (He stops protecting the last chain. Goes full offense.)
  - 4 chains down: *"...fine."* (Triggers Mythic phase early — see below.)

### Phase 2 Ends — The Grab (Mythic Trigger)

**Trigger:** Bitter Breath hits 0 HP **OR** all 4 chains are destroyed (whichever comes first).

> Bitter Breath crumples. His gutting blade clatters from his hand. For a moment — one moment — it's over.
>
> Then his hand shoots out and grabs the nearest intact chain. Or, if all chains are gone, the cage itself.
>
> Orange light flares. Then silver. Then BOTH, swirling together, crawling up his arm like fire under the skin.
>
> He screams. Not in pain. In *hunger.*
>
> His body convulses. His horns crack and regrow — longer, branching, threaded with silver veins. His wounds seal shut, but the new skin is wrong — pale, luminous, shot through with light that doesn't belong in him. His eyes are both silver now. Stolen starlight.
>
> He stands. Taller. His aura isn't rot anymore — it's cold. Clean. Corrupted purity.
>
> He doesn't speak. He just looks at you. And for the first time, you see something in his expression that wasn't there before.
>
> Contempt.

**Stand up. Lower your voice. Tell them directly: "He resets. This is the final phase."**

**Vitality cost of the grab:** -2 Vitality (he tears essence out forcibly). Tick the tokens publicly.

## PHASE 3 — STOLEN STARLIGHT (45 min)

### Bitter Breath Ascendant

**HP:** 140 (reset — fresh pool). **AC:** 19. **Speed:** 50 ft, **fly 30 ft (hover, spectral starlight wings).**

**Damage Immunities (added):** radiant, cold (was resistance).
**Relentless:** SPENT.
**Legendary Resistance:** carry over from Phases 1–2. If he used 3, it's GONE. *Tell the players.* This changes everything for the casters.

**Modified actions:**
- **Multiattack.** 2 Starlight Gutripper + 1 Corrupted Gore (or Starlight Breath if recharged).
- **Starlight Gutripper.** +13, 2d8+7 slashing + 2d6 **radiant.** On crit, target blinded until end of next turn.
- **Corrupted Gore.** +13, 2d10+7 piercing + 2d8 **cold.** DC 17 Dex or BB passes through and attacks again.
- **Starlight Breath (Recharge 5–6, replaces Rotten Breath).** 40-ft cone, DC 17 Con, **6d8 radiant + 4d6 cold,** dazed on fail-by-5. **Costs the Moonkite 1 Vitality each use.**

**Mythic Legendary Actions (every round):**
- **Reposition.** Half speed, no AoO.
- **Starlight Strike.** One Gutripper attack.
- **Nova Rush (2 actions).** Fly full speed. Each creature within 10 ft of path: DC 17 Dex or 3d6 radiant + 2d6 cold. One Gutripper at end.

**Aura of Corrupted Starlight (replaces Aura of Rot):** 15 ft, DC 17 Con or 2d6 cold + speed halved 1 round.

**Unstable Radiance:** Start of his turn, all within 5 ft take 1d6 radiant. **He takes 1d6 too.** Even if the party stalls, he kills himself in ~23 rounds — but the Moonkite dies in 5–8 rounds. The clock favors aggression.

### THE THREE FINAL CHOICES

This is the heart of the whole encounter. **Each PC has an option that is not "attack."** Make sure they each know their option (note-pass at start of Phase 3 if needed).

#### AURORA'S CHOICE — Stabilize vs. Attack

She can use her **action** to stabilize the Moonkite (DC 12 Arcana — easy). **Pauses Vitality loss for 1 round.** That action costs her ~30+ damage and her Hex/Eldritch Blast turn.

**Asimov assist:** If Asimov uses **Psychic Whispers** to link with her, she can stabilize as a **bonus action** instead. *Reward this team play with rich narration.*

**The hidden option:** Aurora can attempt to **call the Moonkite OUT of the cage.** DC 18 Persuasion + spend the Wand of Celestial Warding's last 3 charges as a one-time action. On success, the Moonkite manifests partially OUTSIDE the cage — gains its own initiative, can take ONE action (Healing Light: heal 4d8 to one ally, OR Banishing Beam: 6d6 radiant + Banishment 1 round on Bitter Breath). On failure, the Moonkite loses 2 Vitality from the strain. **Don't tell her this option exists** — let her describe what she's trying to do, and award it if she's clearly trying to communicate with her patron.

#### ASIMOV'S CHOICE — Bounty vs. Betrayal

The Soul Capacitor whispers (private note in Phase 3 round 1):

> *"He's wide open right now. The lamp can drink him mid-fight if you can land the strike. But — Rasheem's earlier offer stands. The bird is dying anyway. If you angle the lamp at IT instead, point-blank, you take the celestial soul. Retirement money. Easy."*

**The Bounty Play (good):** If Asimov delivers the killing blow on Bitter Breath, the Soul Capacitor consumes Bitter Breath's soul (Medium Soul tier, gains Brother's Spite + Stolen Breath + Chain Leash abilities — see Aftermath).

**The Betrayal Play (dark):** Asimov can use his action to point the Soul Capacitor at the cage and roll DC 18 Arcana. On success, the Soul Capacitor begins draining the Moonkite. The Moonkite loses 2 Vitality per round Asimov channels. After 3 rounds: lamp consumes Moonkite, Asimov gains a permanent **Greater Soul** (massive power) — but Aurora's bond shatters, the Moonbow never forms, and the party has a problem with Asimov forever.

**This option exists. Asimov's player decides.** The DM does not editorialize. Simply ask: *"What do you do?"*

#### DRENWAL'S CHOICE — Fight or Fall

Drenwal can attempt the cure HERE, prematurely, by deliberately taking lethal damage and channeling himself to the Moonkite as it dies (or to the Moonkite's energy field while it's still alive). Triggered by:

1. Drenwal voluntarily drops to 0 HP (e.g., refuses healing, tanks a hit, or actively wounds himself).
2. He spends his Channel Divinity to "offer."
3. Roll d100 on the **Cure Table** (DM keeps secret):

| d100 | Result |
|---|---|
| 01–15 | **The Moonkite catches him.** Reborn celestial. Bhaal severed. Permanent loss of Hellrider connection. New cleric subclass features (DM design). Drenwal becomes a Light Cleric of the Moonkite specifically. |
| 16–50 | **A third god catches him.** Roll d6: 1=Selûne, 2=Helm, 3=Tyr, 4=Lathander, 5=Mystra, 6=Ilmater. New patron god, new deity-flavored powers, Bhaal severed but the new god demands service. |
| 51–80 | **No one catches him.** He dies. Standard death saves. If revived later by normal means, Bhaal remains. *No retry.* |
| 81–100 | **Bhaal catches him.** He survives at 1 HP — but Bhaal influence locks at 6/6. Drenwal becomes a Bhaalspawn fully. NPC arc. |

**This is a player decision.** Drenwal's player must explicitly choose to do this. The DM should make it clear it's an option (*"You feel the moment — the Moonkite is reaching for any soul that offers. You could fall now."*) but not push it.

### Lair Actions: Phase 3

1. **Starlight Detonation.** Chain anchor explodes. Within 20 ft of cage: DC 16 Dex or 4d6 radiant + pushed 15 ft.
2. **Structural Collapse.** 10-ft radius (BB chooses): DC 15 Dex or 3d10 bludgeoning + restrained under rubble (DC 15 Athletics to free). Permanent difficult terrain.
3. **Moonkite's Agony.** All in room: DC 15 WIS or incapacitated (move only) until end of next turn. **Aurora auto-succeeds. Bitter Breath auto-fails** (loses next bonus action).
4. **The Palace Shudders.** All on ground: DC 13 Dex or prone. All ground difficult terrain 1 round. BB (flying) unaffected.

### Phase 3 Tactics

Bitter Breath fights like an animal with a god's power. **No tactics. Pure aggression.**

- Round 1: Starlight Breath if recharged → Nova Rush across the room.
- Subsequent: Multiattack closest. Focus Aurora if she stabilizes — *he understands what she's doing.*
- Below 70 HP: Erratic. Crashes into walls. Silver-streaked impact craters. Wild swings (narrative — keep stats same).
- Below 35 HP: **Stops talking entirely.** No banter. Just silence and motion. The stolen light is eating him alive — skin cracks, silver bleeds from fissures.

### Death Throes

> Bitter Breath's knees buckle. The stolen starlight flares one final time — his whole body outlined in silver-white — and then it tears free.
>
> The light rips out of him. Through his horns, his eyes, his wounds. It's not an explosion — it's an exorcism. The Moonkite's power was never his. And it's leaving.
>
> Every creature within 15 ft: **DC 15 CON save or 4d6 radiant + 2d6 cold (half on save).**
>
> The light streams upward and into the cage. The cracked panels glow. The Moonkite absorbs its stolen essence.
>
> Bitter Breath lies on the stone floor. The horns are dark. The silver is gone. He looks... smaller than you remember. Just a demon. Just a brother who wanted to be more.
>
> Silence. Then the cage hums. A single, pure note. Not grief anymore.
>
> **Hope.**

**Vitality:** Restore 2 (the stolen essence returns).

## DM NOTES — RUNNING THIS WELL

1. **Show the Vitality clock from minute one.** Eight tokens on the table. Players need to feel the math.
2. **Roll BB's saves in the open.** Let them see him fail. Let them see him burn LR. Track those tokens.
3. **Don't editorialize the trolley problems.** When BB cuts the infant cage, don't apologize. Describe it. Let the table go quiet. Let them choose.
4. **Reward team play with narration.** Asimov + Aurora's psychic link bonus action — *"For a moment they're one system, rogue and warlock, lamp and wand, feeding power into the cage together."*
5. **The mythic phase transition is your loudest moment.** Stand. Lower voice. *"He resets. This is the final phase."* Then describe slowly.
6. **The Moonkite is a character, not a MacGuffin.** Aurora hears it. Drenwal feels it judge him. Asimov's lamp covets it. Treat its death as a real loss if it happens — don't soften it.
7. **The hostage Hellrider** — if he survives, he's the seed for Drenwal's "die and be reborn" ritual. A Hellrider can perform a death-and-rebirth rite under Tyr or Helm. Plant it now, pay it off in 3 sessions.
8. **Bitter Breath is not a mustache-twirler.** He's a brother who wanted to be more and wasn't. The death should feel sad, not triumphant. Then let the Moonkite's hope fill the silence.
9. **If the fight is too easy:** Add Hostage Strikes more aggressively. Drop Vitality faster (every round in Phase 1).
10. **If the fight is too hard:** Thakk surrenders at half HP. Reduce Siphon Regen to 5/chain. Extend Vitality to 10. The Moonkite reaches out from the cage at Vitality 2 to deal 3d6 radiant to BB on its own initiative — once.
