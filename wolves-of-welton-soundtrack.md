# Wolves of Welton — Session Soundtrack

**Format:** Scene-by-scene cue sheet. Each cue lists 2–3 options (primary + backups) so you can pick what's already in your library or queue what's free on YouTube.

**Default sources:**
- **TA** = Tabletop Audio (free, loopable, ambient + music) — tabletopaudio.com
- **MG** = Michael Ghelfi Studios (YouTube + Spotify, RPG-tuned)
- **AvZ** = Adrian von Ziegler (YouTube, free Celtic/dark fantasy)
- **PG** = Peter Gundry (YouTube/Spotify, mystery + magic)
- **2SFT** = Two Steps From Hell (epic combat)
- **BCS** = Bardify / Battle Bards (YouTube channels)

**Volume rule:** Music sits **under** voice. If a player has to raise their voice to be heard, drop the cue 20%.

## SCENE 5 — THE WOLVES' DEN

### Cue 5.1 — Approaching the Hollow (THE REVEAL)

**Mood:** Eerie wonder. The most important transition of the night. The party sees a settlement, not a den.

**Cut all combat-tonal music. Replace with something that sounds wrong-but-not-hostile.**

| Pick | |
|---|---|
| **Primary** | PG — "The Witching Hour" (slowed) |
| **Backup** | MG — "Mystical Discovery" |
| **Backup** | AvZ — "Mystic Forest" |

**Layer underneath:** TA — "Wind" at low volume for outdoor feel.

### Cue 5.2 — Eavesdropping on Flame & Bolt

**Mood:** Almost silent. The argument IS the audio. Music drops to almost nothing.

## PLAYBACK SETUP

**Two-channel recommended:**
- **Channel A** (music) — Spotify / YouTube / Syrinscape running cue tracks
- **Channel B** (ambient + SFX) — Tabletop Audio's web player or a soundpad app (Soundpad, Mixxx, or Syrinscape)

**Volume targets:**
- Music: just loud enough to hear under conversation
- Ambient: ~40% of music volume
- SFX 1-shots: punch UP to ~120% of music for impact, then fall back

**Crossfading:** A 3–5 second fade between scenes is usually enough. The exceptions are **Cue 1.2** (hard cut to silence), **Cue 5.5** (hard cut + silence), and **Cue 6.2** (hard cut + silence + slam).
