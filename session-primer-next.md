# Session Primer: Post-Dream Sequence

## Opening Scene

The dream machine's smoke clears. Lulu gasps. Mad Maggie's hyena cackle fades. The party is back in the grime of Fort Knucklebone.

**Sensory details:** Iron groaning in the wind. Distant explosions on the horizon. The smell of burning pitch and something sweet — soul coins being melted somewhere in the fort. A redcap scurries past carrying a severed arm (its lunch).

**Remind players:**
- War machine condition and fuel (soul coins)
- Active injuries, spell slots, items
- The dream showed them **three figures** tied to Zariel's fall (Olanthius, Haruman, Bel) and hinted at powerful intermediaries who can find them

## What Mad Maggie Says Next

Maggie is pleased. But she wants payment before doing anything else.

> "The dream machine worked. Good. Now you know the names — Olanthius, Haruman, Bel. Three ghosts of a better time. They know the way to the Bleeding Citadel, but they won't tell you willingly. You'll need to find someone who can **locate** them first."

If pressed for more:

> "Three names for you: **Mordenkainen** — a wizard in a tower who thinks he's smarter than everyone. **Red Ruth** — a hag in the Bone Brambles who drinks memories like wine. And the devil prince **Mephistopheles** — who'll trade you anything if you're willing to pay with your soul."

If asked about the blood cost:

> "Nothing's free in Hell, dearies. These intermediaries won't lift a finger unless you bring them **blood that matters**. Not goblin blood. Not even devil blood. Blood from something that makes the planes tremble."

**Maggie will NOT:**
- Give the Bleeding Citadel location
- Recommend one intermediary over another
- Do more without payment (memories, favors, pain)

## Lulu's State

Emotionally raw. Recovered some memories but fragmented.

**What Lulu remembers (use for RP prompts):**
- Olanthius kneeling among the dead, choosing to walk away — honor twisted into guilt
- Haruman methodically punishing deserters — cruelty wrapped in duty
- Bel watching from his iron throne — calculating, never fighting
- A golden sword falling through fire — and the sound of wings breaking

> Lulu, softly: "They were all there when she fell. They all watched. And none of them stopped it."

**Potential RP moment:** Lulu asks the PCs to promise they won't become like Zariel.

## Three Clear Paths (Present to Players)

### Path A: Tower of Urm (Mordenkainen)
- **Travel:** 10-16 hours by war machine
- **Tone:** Arcane, paranoid, intellectual
- **What to expect:** Spell-trapped halls (DC 17 Arcana), a shield guardian, an impatient wizard who views them as test subjects
- **NPC hints:** Mickey mentions "a mad wizard who talks to himself in a tower that moves"

### Path B: Bone Brambles (Red Ruth)
- **Travel:** 8-14 hours (closest)
- **Tone:** Creepy, visceral, fairy-tale horror
- **What to expect:** Petrified vine maze, undead guardians, a hag who demands memories
- **NPC hints:** Natasha the Dark warns "Red Ruth will take more than she gives — she always does"

### Path C: Mirror of Mephistar (Mephistopheles)
- **Travel:** 10-18 hours
- **Tone:** Political, tempting, ice-cold
- **What to expect:** Ice devils, a frozen mirror-plane, a devil prince who trades favors for knowledge
- **NPC hints:** Barnabas recalls "the mirror shows you what you want — and what you'll lose"

**If players hesitate:** Have NPCs drop hints. If they still can't decide, Maggie says: "Pick one. You can always visit the others later. That's the beauty of Hell — there's always more road."

## Drenwal-Specific Hooks This Session

Drenwal cut the connection. He's safe from tracking. But:
- **Gargauth notices:** The Shield whispers about it.
  > "Smart. You've gone dark. But so has he, Drenwal. Your brother is out there, and now neither of you can find the other. The question is — who's hunting whom?"
- **Dream echoes:** When resting, Drenwal may get a flash — Dumal's melted Hellrider armor, a burning tree with souls nailed to it (Haruman's Hill imagery bleeding into Bhaalspawn visions)

Gargauth is patient. This session, he:
- Offers "helpful" tactical advice during NPC negotiations (actually steering toward deals that benefit him)
- If the party visits the Arches of Ulloch later, he can identify the Nine Hells layers — but demands a promise of freedom first

## Aurora-Specific Hooks This Session

### The Wand Reacts
As they leave Fort Knucklebone, Aurora's **Wand of Celestial Warding** pulses faintly — silver-blue light, gone in a heartbeat. Fire nearby momentarily turns silver.

> If Aurora investigates (DC 14 Arcana): "Something celestial is imprisoned nearby. Not close — but on this plane. It's in pain."

This doesn't point to a specific location yet — it's a persistent pull that grows stronger as she moves through Avernus. It will intensify near locations with infernal war machines (which use Moonkite essence).

## Combat Scaling Reminders (Level 11, 3 Players)

- **Boss fights:** CR 13-16 with legendary/lair actions
- **Standard encounters:** CR 8-11, add minions or hazards
- **Environmental hazards** are your friend — lava, acid, scrap, war machine explosions
- **Action economy:** Always add reactions, lair actions, or minions
- **Moral dilemmas** are the real challenge at this tier — fights alone are too easy
