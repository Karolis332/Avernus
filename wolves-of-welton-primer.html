Design Thesis

The hook is "kill the wolves." The truth is "the wolves are people now."

This oneshot lives or dies on one reveal: the wolves talk. Everything before it is sheep-rustling investigation; everything after it is a negotiated peace under threat of a civil war inside the wolf pack itself.

Three things make it work at the table:
  1. The bounty is suspiciously generous (800 gp for a wolf cull). Hint it loud. Suspicious players investigate; greedy players still get the moral reveal in the cave.
  2. Featherock is the keystone clue. A halfling shepherd, broken, whispering "the wolves were talking." If players skip him, the cave reveal lands cold. If they believe him, they walk in with their swords lowered.
  3. Flame vs Bolt is the real boss fight. Two alpha wolves, one wants peace, one wants slaughter. The party picks which one wins by who they kill, who they spare, and who they talk to.
Win state Wolves dealt with (peace or kill) AND the council pays AND the party can live with what they did. Lying to the council is on the table — Father Merriksonn rolls Insight +5 against Deception.

DM-Only Background

The Truth About the Wolves (do not read aloud)

Three months ago, the village sorcerer Alexi Merriksonn — wielder of Wild Magic — was patrolling the moors and stumbled into a normal wolf pack. He tried to drive them off, rolled a Wild Magic Surge, and was vaporized. The surge dumped his intelligence, language, and a sliver of his elemental power into the surviving wolves.

The pack woke up sapient. They learned Common in days. They built a fence. They drew on cave walls. They drove off the local Owlbear and took its den. They stole sheep — not to slaughter, but to breed. They're trying to live.

The alpha pair, Flame and Bolt, got the most magic — fire breath and lightning breath, respectively. They disagree on what to do next:

  • FLAME Chaotic neutral. Kill humans, terrorize them out of the woods, claim the land.
  • BOLT Lawful neutral. Negotiate or retreat, raise pups, never be hunted again.

Within weeks they will kill each other if the party doesn't intervene.

Nobody in Welton knows any of this. The village thinks it's a normal pack acting weird.

Scene 1 · ~30 min · Outdoor combat

1 · The Welton Wolfpack

Cold Open (read aloud)

The road from the high passes drops down through chalk hills and patchy woods, and below you sits Welton — white-walled houses, smoke from a dozen chimneys, sheep dotting the green like spilled flour. Easy country. Quiet country. Three hundred feet ahead, the road bends between a rock outcrop and the edge of a mossy wood. Two men in shepherd's cloaks are pushing a small flock through the gap, four sheepdogs working the flanks. Then the dogs go rigid. Ears up. Both of them barking at once. The shepherds reach for clubs at their belts — and from both sides of the road, grey shapes erupt out of the brush. Eight wolves. Moving like a single thing.

Roll initiative. Players are 150 ft from the action.

Encounter

8 Wolves vs. 2 Shepherds (Commoner, AC 10, HP 4, club +2 / 1d4) + 4 Sheepdogs (Mastiff) + ~12 sheep.

Wolf Tactics — Critical

The wolves are NOT trying to kill. They are stealing sheep.

  • 4 wolves harass shepherds — bite at legs/arms, no kill blows. Distraction.
  • 4 wolves grab sheep by scruff and bolt west at full speed (50 ft/round).
  • A wolf at >50% HP loss disengages and runs.
  • If 3 wolves drop, the rest break contact and flee west, leaving stolen sheep if needed.
  • No wolf fights to the death here.

Aftermath

If party intervened: shepherds Hennick and Toll are grateful. Walk back to Welton with them, seeded rumors on the way. No reward — poor farmhands.

If party watched: shepherds lose 6–8 sheep, are wounded, beg help to walk back. No hostility.

XP: 400 (8 × 50). Award full even if wolves fled.

Featherock's Room — KEY SCENE

Critical clue scene If players skip Featherock, the den reveal lands cold. If they believe him, they walk into Scene 5 ready to parley.

Willen Featherock, halfling shepherd. Badly wounded weeks ago in the failed posse. White-faced, sweating, bandaged left arm and right leg. Father Merriksonn attends him daily.

Scared. Will not talk freely. Opens up only if the party promises to believe him.

The Story (deliver in fragments)

  1. He was bitten, then trampled by his fleeing companions during the posse ambush.
  2. Drifted in and out of consciousness.
  3. He remembers gruff voices arguing in the darkness and a powerful grip on his ankle.
  4. If pressed gently: "They were talking. The wolves were talking. They were arguing whether to eat me."
  5. He was dumped on the Westly farm doorstep at dawn — alive, deliberately.
  6. Breaks down. Refuses to say more.
Recovery If the wolves' intelligence becomes publicly known, Featherock recovers within days. He'll come find the party to thank them.

Atmosphere (read aloud)

The woods press in tight. Moss-thick trunks, knee-high ferns, the smell of wet earth. Every few minutes something small bolts through the undergrowth — squirrels, rabbits, you can't tell. Once, far off to the west, a single wolf howls. Then nothing. Just the rustle.
Scene 4 · OPTIONAL · ~25 min · Combat filler

4 · The Owlbear's Picnic

Cut this scene if you're tight on time. It foreshadows that wolves drove an Owlbear out of its den.

The Encounter

After ~2h in the woods, the party hears snapping branches. Unless they hide (DEX Stealth DC 15), an Owlbear (modified, see statblock below) crashes into them.

  • Max HP 40 (reduced from 59) — bleeding for weeks
  • Limping, gaunt, half-healed bite wounds clotted with old black blood
  • Scorch marks in the fur (Flame's work)
  • Will not retreat. Starving. Charges easiest target. Fights to 0 HP.

Clues After the Kill

  • WIS (Nature) DC 12: wounds are 2+ weeks old, caused by sharp jaws
  • WIS (Nature) DC 10 OR proficiency: wolves do not pick fights with owlbears
  • If daytime: owlbears are nocturnal — this one was driven from its lair
  • Scorch marks: WIS (Survival) DC 12 — magical heat, not natural fire

XP: 700 (reduced HP makes effective CR ~2.5)

Scene 5 · ~60 min · THE REVEAL

5 · The Wolves' Den

Approach

Cave entrance ~10ft × 10ft in a low grey cliff. Smoke rises from a second hole ~30ft back on the hill above (chimney to the eastern sub-chamber).

Tripwire perimeter at the treeline. WIS Perception DC 13 to spot. Triggers bone noisemakers.

What the Party Sees from the Treeline (read aloud)

Through the trees you see a hollow opening before the cliff. Six wolves. Some lounging by an outdoor fire. Two of them are working on what looks like a wooden fence — with their teeth. One is tearing apart a sheep carcass with surgical care, like it's portioning out meals. Many of them wear strips of bright cloth or beadwork around limbs and necks. The scene is eerily peaceful. Eerily organized. Not a wolf den. A settlement.
Pause here. Let the players notice. Let it land.

The Back Door (Chimney Hole)

The smoke hole on the hill, ~30ft from the cave entrance, opens 15ft above the eastern sub-chamber floor — directly above the meeting-room fire. Big enough for a Small creature (halfling, gnome) or a Medium creature with effort + grappling.

Eavesdroppers will hear Flame and Bolt arguing:

Flame (snarled, hot): "They came armed. They came as hunters. We are weak as long as they think we are animals. Burn the next village. Make them flee. Take the land."
Bolt (low, thunder-soft): "And every season after, more hunters. More fire-throwers like the Lightbringer. We did not become this to die in a glorious last stand. We disappear. We go deep. The pups grow up free."
Flame: "The pups grow up starving if we crawl."

Continue ad-lib until the party acts. The argument lasts 5–10 minutes in-fiction.

Triggering Combat

The wolves panic-bark if: noisemakers trip, stealth checks fail, party walks into the hollow openly. One wolf shouts "HUNTERS!" in growling, halting Common.

All 6 outer wolves attack. After 1 full round, Flame & Bolt emerge from the cave with 4 more armored wolves (+1 AC from leather scraps).

Combat Tactics
  • All wolves intelligent. Aggressive Pack Tactics use.
  • Speed 50ft to outflank squishy casters.
  • Bolt uses Lightning Breath in lines that never catch allies.
  • Flame uses Fire Breath in cones, does not care if 1–2 wolves are caught — trades them for a full party blast.
  • Bite + DC 13 STR save knocks targets prone, then dogpile.

★ THE PEACE TRIGGER

When to trigger

After ANY ONE of these occurs, Bolt roars (thunder-shake the room) and bellows in Common:

Bolt: "HOLD! This fight serves none of us. Speak — or we all die for nothing."
  1. 4 wolves are killed
  2. Flame OR Bolt drops below half HP
  3. Any PC drops to 0 HP

Track this with the tracker above. When any condition is met, the tracker glows.

The party can ignore him. If they do, the wolves fight to the death. If they listen — Scene 6.

Scene 6 · ~30 min · Resolution + climax

6 · Settling the Scores

Branch A — Total War

Party refuses or fails peace. Wolves fight to the death.

  • Nursery wolves beg for pups' lives. Spare pups → surviving wolves vanish forever.
  • Threaten pups → guards fight to the death.
  • Contract fulfilled. Council pays 800 gp.
  • Featherock does not recover. Lives, but haunted.

Branch B — Negotiated Peace

Bolt explains everything:

  • Pack was normal until they brought down "the Lightbringer" (Alexi Merriksonn).
  • Flash of light. They woke up thinking.
  • They hoard food so pups don't starve, elderly aren't abandoned.
  • They drove off the Owlbear.
  • Bolt hates Welton's people but is not Flame — he wants out, not war.
  • His offer: woods + uninhabited western moors → the pack. No raids. Pack kills any other predator threatening Welton. A border.

★ THE FLAME BETRAYAL

Climax — Do not telegraph

The instant it becomes clear Bolt is striking a deal, Flame attacks Bolt from behind.

  • She bites him for massive damage (drop Bolt to ~1/4 HP, take him out of the fight)
  • She screams "COWARD!" in Common, eyes blazing
  • All armored wolves rally to her in a final desperate attack
  • Unarmored wolves fall back in fear and confusion — they don't know who to follow

This is the real boss fight. Flame + 4 armored wolves vs the party, Bolt bleeding on the cave floor.

If party wins AND stabilizes Bolt (DC 10 WIS Medicine or any healing): peace holds. Bolt becomes new alpha.

If Bolt dies in the chaos: peace dies with him. Surviving wolves flee. Sequel seed: a wounded, leaderless intelligent pack vanishes into the wild.

  • Party kills Bolt, lets Flame live: Flame consolidates the pack, starts deliberate murder raids. The village burns inside a month.
  • Party negotiates, then betrays: Bolt's pack hunts them across the region. Pack tactics + 2 elemental alphas. The province becomes hostile.
  • Party tells the whole village the truth: Welton splits. Half want peace, half want extermination. A second sorcerer arrives within a year. The wolves are either allies or conquerors.
  • A PC dies to Flame's Fire Breath: Bolt offers, in genuine remorse, to negotiate a body return and concessions. He won't undo the death but he'll make it mean something.
