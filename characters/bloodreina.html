YOU ARE BLOODREINA

A welcome for a first-time player. Read this once. Then forget the rules and remember the woman.

This booklet is twenty pages of her. Every page is a level. Every level is a moment in her story — the girl hidden beneath the floor, the wild thing in the forest, the warrior in the pit, the queen in the bunker, and what she becomes when survival costs everything. You don't need to memorize numbers to play her. You need to know who she is. The dice will follow.

The Three Things That Matter

  1. Speak as her. Not "my character says" — just say it. Quiet. Slow. Certain. People who shout are afraid. She is not afraid.
  2. When she's angry, you Rage. Mechanically: bonus action, free, lasts a minute. Narratively: stand up at the table. Lean forward. The room belongs to you now.
  3. She does not fall. The whole class is built around refusing to die. Lean into it. Charge the dragon. Stand in front of the wizard. The mechanics will catch you.

If You Only Remember Four Words

Rage. Reckless. Swing. Survive.

  • Rage = your bonus action almost every fight. Just say "I rage."
  • Reckless = "I attack recklessly." You get advantage. They get advantage back. You don't care.
  • Swing = "I attack with my greatsword." Two attacks at L5.
  • Survive = if you go down, ask the DM: "Do I get to use Relentless Endurance?" The answer is usually yes.

When in Doubt, Ask Yourself

"What would scare her enemies most?"

Then do that. Walking forward when others retreat. Speaking softer when others shout. Pulling the spear out herself. Theatre is half the build.

THE VOICE

How she speaks. How she moves. What to say at the table.

Vocal Pattern

  • Low register. Drop your voice half an octave below your normal speaking pitch.
  • Slow tempo. Half-speed. Make people wait. Make them lean in.
  • No filler. No "um, like, sort of." If you don't have a word, say nothing.
  • Direct eye contact. Hold it one beat longer than feels natural.
  • End sentences flat. No upward inflection. Statements, not questions.

Body at the Table

  • When she rages: stand up. Put both hands flat on the table.
  • When she's listening: don't fidget. Don't nod. Just watch.
  • When she's threatened: lean toward the threat, not away.
  • When she gives an order: don't repeat it. They heard.
  • Touch the hilt of your imaginary sword when you think.

Lines to Steal — English

"My name is Octavia. And I am not afraid."introducing yourself once, never again
"Choose."after offering exactly two options
"You are Wonkru, or you are the enemy of Wonkru."before anyone is allowed to leave the room
"Strong hearts."greeting between allies — quiet, gripping their forearm
"The body is the gift."before a kill she does not want to make
"We do what we must."after a kill she did not want to make
"Get knocked down. Get back up."to a friend bleeding on the ground
"I am Bloodreina."only ever to enemies — never allies

Lines in Trigedasleng (the Grounder tongue)

Pronounce it. Don't translate at the table — let people figure it out. Mystery is the queen's friend.

Ai laik Skairipa.
I am the killer of sky people.

Your fight is not over.
Jus drein jus daun.
Blood must have blood.
Omon gon oson.
All for one.
Heda kom Wonkru.
Commander of one people.
Bloodreina nou stedaun.
Bloodreina does not fall.
Oso na throu daun ogeda.
We will fight together.

The Rituals That Are Hers

  • Before every battle: she paints the red horizontal band across both eyes with two fingers. Slow. In silence.
  • Before every meal she eats first. Always. It is law.
  • When someone dies under her command, she says — quietly, only to them — "Yu gonplei ste odon." (Your fight is over.)
  • She never sits when her people stand. She never stands when her people kneel.

CHARACTER SHEET

I

The Girl Under the Floor

Sixteen years hidden. One night to learn she is dangerous.

You were born somewhere you were never supposed to exist. For sixteen years the world above the floorboards was a rumor — boots, voices, mealtimes you could not have. They find you. They cage you. They drop you, with ninety-nine other prisoners, into a forest of green and rot and freedom. You kill your first man with a sharpened rock. Your hands do not shake. You learn something about yourself that night you do not yet have a word for.
"My name is Octavia.
And I am not afraid." — your first words in daylight

What You Gain

Rage 2 per long rest
Bonus action. For 1 minute: +2 damage with strength-based weapons, resistance to most weapon damage (you take half!), advantage on strength checks and saves. Ends if you don't attack or take damage on your turn.
Unarmored Defense
When you wear no armor: AC = 10 + DEX mod + CON mod. You may still use a shield. (For now, wear the chain shirt — it's a bigger number.)
Tough Variant Human feat
+2 HP per level. Right now: +2 HP. At Level 20: +40 HP. This is the simplest, most powerful survivability feat in the game. Bloodreina does not die because she has more HP than the math says is possible.
At the Table

When the DM asks who goes first into the unknown, you say: "I go first." No qualifications. No "I think I should." Just the statement.

What You Can Do

Walk into a fight outnumbered and walk out alive. Resistance halves most weapon damage. You aren't a soldier yet — you're a wild thing the world has not learned to fear.

II

We're Back, Bitches

First blood. First love. First lesson: the forest takes.

The hundred run wild. You run wildest. A grounder pulls you from a river and for the first time in your life — you choose not to stab him. He teaches you three words: kom Trikru. Yu gonplei. The forest teaches you to swing a blade if you are willing to bleed for the lesson. You are.
"Whatever the hell we want." — motto of the wild year

What You Gain

Reckless Attack
Once per turn, declare an attack reckless: advantage on every melee strength attack this turn, but enemies have advantage against you until your next turn. This becomes the engine of every fight you take.
Danger Sense
Advantage on DEX saves against effects you can see — fire breath, fireballs, traps. (No advantage if blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.)
At the Table

Almost every attack from now on: declare it reckless. The defensive trade is fake — you have resistance, Tough, and (soon) revival features. Always swing first, swing hardest, never blink.

What You Can Do

Throw yourself into a fight knowing you'll come out the other side. Charge through fire. Trade defense for damage, every turn, on purpose.

III

I Am Trikru

The forest claims you. The blood gods notice.

Indra watches you train and does not laugh anymore. The clan paints a stripe of mud across your cheek and gives you a real blade. Your rage finds a god to listen — not a kind one. Your sword starts to hum on the first strike of every fight. The grounders call it Divine Fury. You call it justice.
"Ai laik Trikru nau.
I am not a Sky person anymore." — your oath to the second clan

Path of the Zealot subclass chosen

Divine Fury
While raging, the first creature you hit with a weapon attack each turn takes +1d6 + ½ Barbarian level radiant or necrotic damage (your choice — pick necrotic, it fits her).
Warrior of the Gods
A spell that restores you to life (e.g., raise dead, revivify) needs no costly material components. Your gods want you back on the field.
At the Table

Every fight, your first strike deals bonus dark energy. Describe it aloud: "The blade hums. Where it lands, the wound smokes."

What You Can Do

Open every combat with a sword strike that drips holy fire or rot. Die in a cause that won't let you stay dead.

IV

The Indra Years

Get knocked down. Get back up. Knock harder.

A year of beatings from a woman who refuses to call you by your name. You come out of it lean as a wolf and twice as patient. You still cannot beat Indra. You are no longer trying to.
"Get knocked down. Get back up." — Lincoln's voice, in your head, every morning

Ability Score Improvement #1

Choose one
  • +2 STR — raise STR by 2. Hit harder. Hit more often.
  • Sentinel feat — enemies attacking your allies provoke an opportunity attack from you; your OAs reduce target speed to 0 on hit. Turn yourself into a wall.
  • +1 STR / +1 CON — balanced; both saves and HP rise slightly.

Recommended for Bloodreina flavor: Sentinel. The queen never lets anyone past her.

What You Can Do

Either raw damage, or absolute control of your ten-foot circle. Either way, your enemies are working harder than you are.

V

Skairipa

The Conclave. Twelve champions. One survivor.

The bombs are coming. The bunker holds three hundred. Each clan sends one champion to the arena and only the last alive earns the door. You win by the eleventh body. They paint a red infinity across your eyes and call you Skairipa — Killer of Sky People. You do not let them see you cry.
"Ai laik Skairipa.
Kom Skaikru." — declared as the last sword falls

What You Gain

Extra Attack
When you take the Attack action, you make two attacks instead of one. Damage per turn nearly doubles.
Fast Movement
While in no armor, light, or medium: speed +10 ft (now 40 ft). You close distance before your enemies can think.
At the Table

This is the level you stop being "promising" and start being scary. After this fight, paint the infinity on. Earn the name in front of the party. Make it a moment.

What You Can Do

Two reckless attacks per turn with advantage, plus Divine Fury on the first hit. You are now, statistically, more dangerous than anything CR-equivalent in the Monster Manual.

VI

The Will of Iron

The bunker closes. The years begin.

Twelve clans, one shelter, five years of dark. You walk the corridors at night to remind people you exist. Someone tries to charm you into stepping aside. They die first. They do not die last.
"We will live." — spoken once. Believed by all.

What You Gain

Fanatical Focus
If you fail a saving throw while raging, you may reroll it once. Once per rage. Your gods, or your stubbornness, will not allow defeat.
What You Can Do

Shrug off mind-control, sleep magic, fear, paralysis — once per rage, the dice get a do-over. Bosses fear your saves now.

VII

One People

Twelve banners. One sigil. One word.

You gather the clans in the great hall and burn their separate flags. From the ashes you paint a single red infinity on the wall and name them all Wonkru. Some weep. Some kneel. Some are quietly removed.
"One people.
One law.
One queen." — the founding decree

What You Gain

Feral Instinct
Advantage on initiative. If you are surprised at the start of combat, you may immediately enter rage and act normally on your first turn. Nothing catches you sleeping anymore.
What You Can Do

Almost always strike first. Ambushes become your ambushes. You are the first sword to swing in any fight you are in.

VIII

Strong Hearts

Year two underground. Discipline forged in stone.

You train them. Every morning. Every evening. The children watch you swing the great sword in the corridor and the corridor smells of sweat and oil and a kind of love you will not name. You kiss no one. You drink alone. You are becoming something you cannot return from.
"Strong hearts." — greeting between Wonkru

Ability Score Improvement #2

Recommended: Great Weapon Master feat
  • When you score a critical hit OR reduce a creature to 0 HP with a melee attack, bonus action: one extra melee attack.
  • Before any melee attack with a heavy weapon, you may take −5 to the attack roll for +10 damage.

Combined with Reckless Attack (advantage cancels the −5 penalty), your average damage per round leaps from ~22 to ~36.

What You Can Do

Cleave through groups. Drop one enemy, swing immediately at the next. Become the answer to every encounter with more than three weak enemies.

IX

The Pit Remembers

Dissent rises. The pit is reopened.

A man speaks against you in the great hall. The corridor goes quiet. You do not have him hanged. You walk him to the pit. He fights well. You fight better. The next dissenter waits four months to speak — and speaks softer.
"The strong rule.
The weak feed them." — law of the pit

What You Gain

Brutal Critical (1 die)
On a critical hit with a melee weapon, you roll one extra weapon damage die.
What You Can Do

A critical greatsword hit now rolls 3d6 + STR + rage, then GWM grants a bonus action attack. Single crit, dead boss.

X

The Red Queen Speaks

Wonkru rallies. The world bends.

The bunker is yours in name and in fact. When you shout an order, men who outweigh you by sixty pounds run faster. When you pause, the corridor holds its breath. This is not love. This is gravity.
"Omon gon oson.
All for one." — battle cry of Wonkru

What You Gain

Zealous Presence 8 / long rest
Bonus action. Up to 10 allies within 60 ft that can hear you gain advantage on all attack rolls and saving throws until the start of your next turn. A round of party-wide superiority. This is your commander aura.
At the Table

When you use this, shout the command aloud: "OMON GON OSON!" The whole table responds. The encounter changes shape.

What You Can Do

Turn one round of any fight into a slaughter. Pair with the wizard's Hypnotic Pattern or the cleric's Spirit Guardians to break encounters open.

XI

The Dark Year Begins

The hydrofarm fails. Three hundred mouths. No food.

Soy reaches a height of one inch and yellows. Calculations are run. Re-run. Whispered. There is not enough. There will not be enough. The doctor brings you the math at midnight and you do not look surprised. You have been waiting.
"We do what we must.
Or we die." — spoken to no one, to yourself

What You Gain

Relentless Rage
When reduced to 0 HP while raging and not killed outright, make a CON save (DC 10). On success, you drop to 1 HP instead. DC increases by 5 each time you use this until a short or long rest.
What You Can Do

You can now take a second near-fatal hit in a single combat and remain standing. The math says you should be dead. You aren't.

XII

The Body Is the Gift

The Dark Year ends. Wonkru survives. Something else dies.

You sit at the long table with the rest of them and lift the first portion to your mouth. The hall is quiet enough to hear the swallow. Nobody refuses after that. Nobody speaks of it again. There is no word for what they did, so they invent one: the body is the gift. The girl beneath the floor would not recognize you.
"The body is the gift.
And I eat first." — year three of the bunker, day forty of the famine

Ability Score Improvement #3

Recommended: +2 STR → cap at 20
Maximum mortal strength. Attack bonus and damage rise by +1. You hit on a 6 against most armored enemies.
At the Table

This is the level where you should ask the DM if you may roleplay a moment. The first meal of the dark year. The cost. It is the pivot of the saga. Don't skip it.

What You Can Do

Damage per round at L12 with rage + GWM + Divine Fury reaches ~45 average. Bosses cannot ignore you now.

XIII

You Are Wonkru

Or you are the enemy of Wonkru. Choose.

The bunker opens. The world above is bone and ash and a sliver of green. You ride out at the head of an army that has forgotten softness. They will not negotiate. You do not know how anymore.
"You are Wonkru,
or you are the enemy of Wonkru.
Choose." — the only sentence you speak for a week

What You Gain

Brutal Critical (2 dice)
Critical melee hits roll two extra weapon damage dice. A greatsword crit now rolls 4d6 dice before mods.
What You Can Do

A critical hit can drop a CR 8 enemy outright. With Reckless Attack your crit rate is ~9.75% per swing — once every two-three rounds, statistically. Crits become events.

XIV

Bloodreina Does Not Fall

The legend hardens into law.

You take a spear through the side at the edge of the bone valley and walk back to camp with it still in you. The healer pulls it out. You do not sit down for the operation. The story is told by morning. By evening it is law.
"Bloodreina nou stedaun."
(Bloodreina does not fall.) — Wonkru proverb, year four

Path of the Zealot Capstone

Rage Beyond Death
While raging, having 0 HP does not knock you unconscious. You still make death saves and suffer their effects, but you do not fall. You die only if rage ends while you're below 0 HP.
What You Can Do

Functionally cannot be killed mid-rage by ordinary means. Walk through the dragon's breath. Take the spell. Keep swinging. This is the most terrifying martial feature in 5e.

XV

Persistent Rage

The fury never lets you go.

Sleep comes harder now. Even at rest your hand sits on the sword. You rage and the rage does not loosen its grip — it stays even if you walk away from the kill, even if you sit beside the fire and pretend to listen. There is no quiet in you anymore.
"I am always angry.
I have been since I was twelve." — told to Indra, once, late

What You Gain

Persistent Rage
Your rage never ends early from not attacking or not being hit. It lasts the full minute (or until you fall unconscious or choose to end it).
What You Can Do

Use Rage as a movement / scouting / non-combat resource. Walk into the king's hall raging. Sit through the ambassador's speech raging. You are never not ready.

Reckoning

You see, for the first time, what you have done.

The mirror catches you in the war-tent at dusk and you do not recognize the woman in it. The paint is dry on your cheekbones. Your brother left a season ago and has not come back. Somewhere in the bone valley a child is calling your name in their sleep — the wrong way, the queen way. You close your eyes.
"I am the monster you made." — spoken to your brother, in a dream

Ability Score Improvement #4

Recommended: Resilient (WIS) feat
  • +1 WIS.
  • Proficiency in WIS saves. Adds prof bonus (+5 at this tier) to every wisdom save.

Plugs your one save weakness — charms, fears, dominations. The queen does not get told what to do.

What You Can Do

Resist mind-control magic that would otherwise turn a barbarian into a problem. Fanatical Focus still rerolls one save per rage on top — you become nearly immune to mental attacks.

XVII

The Apex

Nothing alive matches you in the dance of blades.

Champions are sent. None return. A general from across the sea offers a duel of honor and you accept and the duel lasts nine seconds and the general's belt buckle is on your armor by week's end. The world begins to send messengers instead of warriors.
"Yu gonplei nou ste odon."
(Your fight is not over.) — spoken to the fallen, never to yourself

What You Gain

Brutal Critical (3 dice)
Critical hits roll three extra weapon damage dice. A greatsword crit: 5d6 + STR + rage + GWM + Divine Fury. Average ~54 damage on a single attack. Twice per turn possible.
What You Can Do

End encounters in one turn. A critical hit on the boss is no longer "lucky" — it's scheduled.

XVIII

Indomitable Might

You lift what cannot be lifted. You break what cannot be broken.

The gate of the dead city is twenty feet of riveted iron. You put your shoulder to it. It does not budge. You put your shoulder to it again. The hinge weeps. The second hinge weeps. The third hinge gives. Inside, the bone king is already on his knees.
"Nothing holds me." — first words spoken inside the dead city

What You Gain

Indomitable Might
If your total for a STR check is less than your STR score, you may use your STR score (20) as the result instead. You never roll a STR check below 20.
What You Can Do

Auto-succeed every STR check under DC 20. Grapple anything humanoid. Lift the portcullis. Pull a friend up the cliff. Bend the iron bars. Mythic feats become routine.

XIX

The Bridge

One last choice: the queen, or the woman.

Your brother is at the gate, alive after all. There is a child with him. The child is afraid of you. The child should not be afraid of you. You kneel in the dust of the road and the child does not run. You are, for a heartbeat, the girl beneath the floor again. Then you stand.
"I am Octavia.
Of the Trikru.
Of Wonkru.
Of the Sky People." — last public speech as Heda

Ability Score Improvement #5

Choose one
  • +2 CON → cap at 20 — more HP, better unarmored AC, concentration if anyone ever forces it. Recommended.
  • Polearm Master feat — only if you've swapped to a glaive/halberd.
  • Mobile feat — speed +10 (now 50 ft) and no OAs from creatures you've attacked.

Primal Champion

The apex. The end. The beginning of the next thing.

You are the strongest mortal in the bone valley. The strongest mortal in the bunker. The strongest mortal your gods have ever bothered to look at. Whether you keep the crown, set it down, or hand it to the child at the gate — the world will turn around your decision. You know this. You are, for the first time in many years, not in a hurry.
"I am the bridge.
Cross, or do not cross." — final line of the saga

Capstone

Primal Champion
STR increases by 4, to a maximum of 24. CON increases by 4, to a maximum of 24.
  • STR 24 → +7 mod. Attack & damage: +7.
  • CON 24 → +7 mod. +80 HP from CON alone (4 × 20 levels). Unarmored AC base: 17.
  • Rage bonus damage: +4. Brutal Critical: +3 dice. Divine Fury at L20: +1d6 + 10.
What You Can Do

Single-target damage on a crit: ~62 average. Two attacks plus GWM bonus action = potential ~110 damage in one turn. HP pool with Tough: ~280. You are, in mechanical fact, a small god.

EVERY TURN

When the dice come out, do this. In this order.

  1. Not raging? Bonus action → "I rage."
  2. Move up to your speed toward the most dangerous enemy.
  3. Action: "I attack — recklessly. Greatsword. With GWM."
  4. Roll to hit (twice — advantage). Take the higher.
  5. If it hits: roll 2d6 + STR mod + rage bonus (+ 10 from GWM). First hit each turn: also roll Divine Fury (1d6 + half level).
  6. Second attack: same routine.
  7. If anything died or you crit: bonus action → one more attack.
  8. Reaction: opportunity attack on enemies leaving your reach. Sentinel: also when they hit allies.
  9. At 0 HP: at L11+, CON save for Relentless Rage. At L14+, just keep going — Rage Beyond Death.

THE FIGHT

Barbarian combat — what to do, what to roll, every turn

You are big. You are angry. You hit things. Read top to bottom — it works on round one and round ten.

STEP 1 — START OF YOUR TURN

Ask yourself If YES Do this
Am I raging?No, but I want toBonus action: RAGE. Resistance to slashing/piercing/bludgeoning. +2 damage on STR melee. Advantage on STR checks/saves. Lasts 1 minute.
Below half HP?YesRage if not already. Use Relentless Endurance (lvl 1 racial if Half-Orc) or your big hit-back move.
Did I take damage and survive?YesRage keeps you up: resistance means halve incoming damage. Trust it.

STEP 2 — MOVE

Walk up to 30 ft toward the meanest thing. Use cover if archers shoot you. Difficult terrain → half speed. You can break it into chunks: 15 ft → attack → 15 ft.

STEP 3 — ATTACK (Action)

Roll Dice Add
To Hit (greatsword/greataxe)d20+ STR mod + Proficiency
Damage — greatsword2d6+ STR mod + 2 (Rage)
Damage — greataxe1d12+ STR mod + 2 (Rage)
Crit (nat 20)Double the dice2d6 → 4d6 + mods
Reckless Attack (free)Advantage on STR attacks this turn. Enemies hit YOU with advantage until your next turn. Use when you NEED the hit.

STEP 4 — EXTRA ATTACK (level 5+)

Same action, swing again. Two attacks per Attack action. Level 5 onward.

STEP 5 — BONUS ACTION OPTIONS

RageEnter rage (if not raging).
Frenzy attack (Berserker, while raging)One extra melee weapon attack. Costs exhaustion at end of rage.
Dash / Disengage / HideOnly if you can't make a useful attack. Disengage = no opportunity attack.

REACTIONS — (Not your turn)

Opportunity AttackEnemy leaves your reach without Disengaging? Free melee swing.
Rage TriggerIf you take damage on someone else's turn, that counts for staying raged.

ADVANTAGE / DISADVANTAGE

AdvantageRoll 2d20, take the higher. Reckless Attack gives this on STR attacks.
DisadvantageRoll 2d20, take the lower. Cancels with advantage (don't stack).

CRITICAL HIT / MISS

Natural 20 on attackDouble the damage dice. Greatsword: 2d6 becomes 4d6 + mods. Always hits.
Natural 1 on attackAutomatic miss, no matter the bonus.

RAGE CHECKLIST — before you swing

  • ☐ I am raging (bonus action used or already up)
  • ☐ I am using a melee STR weapon (the +2 only works in melee, no ranged)
  • ☐ I am NOT wearing heavy armor (rage turns off if I am)
  • ☐ I attacked OR took damage this turn (rage drops after one round of nothing happening)
  • ☐ Reckless? Decide BEFORE you roll the d20
