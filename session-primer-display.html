Act 1: Departure from the Tower
Estimated time: 1 hour
Opening Scene
The real Mordenkainen watches from a balcony of the second Tower of Urm as the party leaves. He does not wave. He does not call out. He simply observes — then the tower shimmers and shifts position, as if it was never there.
Sensory Details
The air smells of ozone and old books. The wasteland stretches before them — cracked obsidian plains, distant fires, the constant low rumble of the Blood War. Somewhere to the south, a river cuts through the landscape — darker than the Styx, thick, almost alive.
Rasheem's Reward — Bloodbath Bounty Complete

Shortly after departing the Tower, the Soul Capacitor on Asimov's belt begins to hum. Purple light seeps from the lamp's spout. Rasheem's voice — smooth, theatrical, deeply self-satisfied — fills the air around Asimov.

Rasheem

"Well, well, well. Look who's been busy. Bloodbath the Drinker of Gore. CR fifteen-and-change. Consumed, not killed — I have to say, that's style, Asimov. The syndicate appreciates style."

The lamp vibrates. Purple crystalline veins along its surface pulse and branch — new channels forming in real time, the psionic circuitry evolving.

Rasheem

"The soul's been processed. The Capacitor's been upgraded. You can handle bigger game now — medium-grade souls. CR five through ten. The lamp will drink deeper, and you'll hit harder. Consider it a bonus."

Rasheem pauses. His tone shifts — still charming, but with an edge.

Rasheem

"Now. About your next contract. I've got something cooking. Something significant. But not yet. You'll know when I call. For now... enjoy the upgrade. And Asimov? Try not to die before I get my commission."

The lamp goes quiet. The new purple channels glow faintly, then settle.

Mechanical Reward

Soul Capacitor evolves — Medium Souls tier unlocked (see soul-capacitor.md)

  • Medium Souls (CR 5–10): Psionic Surge (+2d8 psychic + 1 bonus SA die), Soul Shiv (reaction, 3d6 psychic + frightened), Memory Siphon, Soul Mask, Psionic Echo
  • Soul storage: 2 slots (prof +4, half rounded down)
  • Rasheem hints at a future Major Soul bounty contract — seed for later sessions
Roleplay Note
This is Asimov's spotlight moment. Let the player read the new abilities. Let the other PCs react — this lamp just evolved in front of them. Aurora's wand pulses in response (celestial magic recognizing psionic energy). Drenwal's Shield might whisper something jealous: "Interesting toy. Mine is better."
Party Discussion Time
Let This Breathe
The party has two major revelations to process. Give them space.
  • Drenwal's cure — "Die and be reborn in the eyes of another god." Let the players discuss what this means. Don't answer it for them. Gargauth will weigh in (see below).
  • The Moonkite — They have the key, they have directions. Aurora should feel urgency. The wand is pulsing more frequently now.
  • Fallen Three info — Mordenkainen gave them locations. This is the MAIN QUEST thread, but the Moonkite is the IMMEDIATE personal quest. Let the party decide priority. If they hesitate, Aurora's wand pulses hard — silver light, almost painful — pulling south toward the river of blood.
Gargauth Speaks (While Traveling)

The Shield whispers to Drenwal about Mordenkainen's words:

Gargauth (The Shield)

"Die and be reborn? How poetic. How... convenient for whatever god catches you on the way down."

"Think carefully, Drenwal. When you die — and you will die, if you go through with this — your soul will be untethered for a heartbeat. One heartbeat. And in that heartbeat, every god and devil and demon lord who's ever tasted your blood will reach for you."

"Bhaal will reach. I promise you that. And if no one else catches you first... well. You know what he'll make of you."

If Drenwal asks Gargauth for help:

Gargauth (The Shield)

"I could catch you. When you fall. I could hold your soul and give it back — clean, free, mine. But that's a conversation for later. First, survive today."

DM Note
Gargauth is planting the seed that HE could be the god who "receives" Drenwal in the rebirth. This is the trap option. Don't push it — just let it sit.
Bhaal Influence Check

Drenwal hasn't killed anyone meaningful recently. The Hunger mechanic:

The Hunger
  • If it's been 3+ in-game days since a kill: DC 15 WIS save or gain 1 exhaustion level
  • Dreams become worse — flash of Dumal burning a village, Bhaal's laughter
  • The Shield resists his grip slightly (disadvantage on Shield-related checks until he feeds the Hunger)
Travel Encounter: The Smiler Proposition
Medium Difficulty — RP + Possible Combat

4 hours into the journey, the party spots a war machine parked sideways across a narrow canyon pass. Painted on the side in blood: a grinning face. Smiler the Defiler is waiting for them.

Smiler is NOT here to fight. He's here to make a deal.

Smiler the Defiler

"Well, well! The Bloodbath-killers! I heard what you did. Impressive. Stupid, but impressive."

"Here's the thing — Bloodbath's little brother Bitter Breath? He's been consolidating. Took the Palace, took the troops, took the throne. And he's angry. Very angry."

"Now, I've got a problem with Bitter Breath too. He's been raiding my supply lines. Killing my people. So I'm thinking... maybe we have a common enemy?"

Smiler's Offer
  • He'll give them a map to the Palace of Gore with guard rotations and a secret entrance (a collapsed drainage tunnel on the east side)
  • In exchange: when the party kills Bitter Breath, Smiler gets the Palace and all its forces
  • DC 16 Insight: Smiler is being genuine about wanting Bitter Breath dead, but he's also holding back information — he knows about the Moonkite's value and wants to claim it
Outcomes

If the party accepts: They get advantage on scouting the Palace (see Act 3). Smiler drives off cackling.

If the party refuses: Smiler shrugs. "Your funeral. Literally." He drives off. No map, no secret entrance. The party must scout the Palace themselves.

If the party attacks: Smiler has Counterspell ready. He uses his war machine to flee (speed 100 ft). He becomes a future enemy who warns Bitter Breath they're coming.

Aurora's Wand — Intensifying

As they travel south following the river of blood, the Wand of Celestial Warding reacts:

  • 1 hour in: Faint pulse. Silver glow.
  • 3 hours in: Steady pulse, like a heartbeat. Aurora feels warmth in her chest.
  • 5 hours in (approaching Palace): The wand is VIBRATING. Silver-blue light spills through her pack. Aurora can hear something — not words, but a song. Faint, agonized, beautiful. The Moonkite is calling.
DC 12 Arcana (Easy — You Want Her to Succeed)
"The Moonkite is close. It's in terrible pain. It's being... drained. Something is feeding on it."
Act 2: The River of Blood
Estimated time: 45 minutes
Description

The "river of blood" is not metaphorical. It's a literal tributary of gore that feeds into the River Styx — 20 feet wide, slow-moving, thick as syrup. The banks are crusted with dried crimson. The smell is overwhelming — rotting meat, iron, bile.

The river doesn't flow like water. It pulses. Like a vein. Like something vast and wounded is bleeding somewhere upstream, and this is where it drains.
Environmental Hazard: The River

Touching the river: DC 14 CON save or be poisoned for 1 hour (vomiting, weakness).

Falling in: DC 14 CON save or take 3d6 necrotic damage per round and gain 1 level of exhaustion on exit.

Following the River (Exploration)

The party follows the river for 2–3 hours. Along the way:

Landmark 1 — The Bone Dam (0:30 in)

A crude dam made of bones and war machine wreckage partially blocks the river. Bodies of demons and hobgoblins are tangled in it.

DC 14 Investigation
These are Bloodbath's old forces — killed by Bitter Breath during his takeover. Some were executed (clean cuts), some were drowned in the river. Bitter Breath purged anyone still loyal to his brother.
Landmark 2 — The Warning Totems (1:00 in)

Poles driven into the riverbank every 50 feet, each topped with a severed head (demon, hobgoblin, and one human — a Hellrider). The heads are still alive — mouths opening and closing silently.

DC 15 Religion (Drenwal)
These are soul-bound sentinels. They can't speak, but if anyone approaches the Palace without Bitter Breath's permission, the heads scream — alerting the garrison.
Solution
Destroy them quietly (AC 10, 15 HP each, but make noise when destroyed — DC 14 Stealth per totem) OR use Silence spell to pass through without triggering them.
Landmark 3 — Bitter Breath's War Camp (1:30 in)

Before the Palace itself, there's an outer camp. 20+ hobgoblin soldiers, 4 maw demons, cooking fires, crude barricades. This is NOT the target — this is the garrison.

  • DC 16 Stealth (group) to bypass the camp entirely
  • DC 15 Perception to spot patrol gaps (advantage on group Stealth)
  • If detected: 8 hobgoblins + 2 maw demons scramble. This is NOT the main fight — run it fast (3–4 rounds max) or let the party flee toward the Palace
Scouting the Palace of Gore

The Palace is a squat, brutal fortress built into a cliff face above the river. It's made of blackite stone and fused bone. Gore drips from every surface — not decorative, structural. The walls are held together with dried blood and demonic resin.

Key Features Visible from Outside
  • Main gate: Heavy, guarded by 4 hobgoblin veterans (AC 18, 55 HP each). Iron portcullis.
  • East drainage tunnel: (Only known if Smiler told them) A collapsed section of wall where the river of blood used to flow through. DC 15 Athletics to clear rubble, leads directly into the lower level.
  • Upper terrace: Open-air platform where Bitter Breath surveys his domain. Reachable by climbing (DC 18 Athletics) or flying.
  • Silver glow: Aurora can see — faintly — a pulsing silver light deep inside the Palace. The Moonkite.
Act 3: Palace of Gore — Infiltration
Estimated time: 1.5 hours
Palace Layout (3 Key Areas)
Lower Level — The Drainage Tunnels
  • Knee-deep in old blood (difficult terrain)
  • 2 maw demons (CR 5, 137 HP each) patrol here — they feed on the blood
  • Traps: Pressure plates trigger bone spear traps (DC 15 Perception to spot, DC 14 Dex save, 3d8 piercing)
  • Leads to: the Throne Room (up a ramp) or the Prison Level (down a passage)
Main Level — The Throne Room
  • Where Bitter Breath holds court
  • Bloodbath's old throne — a massive chair made of fused weapons and bones. Bitter Breath has added hobgoblin banners.
  • 6 hobgoblin soldiers (AC 18, 55 HP) stand guard
  • 2 barbed devils (AC 15, 110 HP) — Bitter Breath's new infernal allies
  • Bitter Breath is HERE unless the party is sneaking (in which case he's on the upper terrace)
Prison Level — The Moonkite's Cage
  • Down a spiraling passage carved from the cliff
  • The air gets colder and cleaner as they descend — the Moonkite's aura fighting back against the corruption
  • 1 chain devil (CR 8, 85 HP) guards the cage. It holds the second key (redundant)
  • The Cage: A 10-foot sphere of hellforged adamantine, chains running through it into the walls. Inside, a faint silver-blue glow pulses weakly. The Moonkite.
Infiltration Options
Option A: Stealth (Drainage Tunnel)
  • Enter through east tunnel (DC 15 Athletics)
  • Sneak past or kill maw demons (DC 16 Stealth with blood-terrain disadvantage unless they find dry paths)
  • Reach Prison Level without alerting Bitter Breath
  • Risk: If detected, Bitter Breath arrives in 2 rounds with hobgoblin reinforcements
Option B: Diplomacy / Deception
  • Approach the main gate. Claim to be mercenaries, traders, or Smiler's envoys
  • DC 18 Deception to get past hobgoblin gate guards
  • DC 20 Deception to fool Bitter Breath himself (he's paranoid after his brother's death)
  • If they mention Bloodbath: Bitter Breath's demeanor shifts to cold fury. DC goes up by 2.
Option C: Assault
  • Charge the front gate. Fight through hobgoblins, then throne room, then find the prison.
  • This is the hardest path — multiple fights with no rest between them.
  • Only viable if the party is fully resourced.
The Moral Dilemma: Captain Grozz

In the drainage tunnel (or wherever the party infiltrates), they encounter Captain Grozz — a hobgoblin officer who is clearly afraid. He's been ordered to guard the lower level but knows Bitter Breath will kill him for any failure.

Captain Grozz

"Please — I don't want to die for this place. Bloodbath was cruel but predictable. His brother is... worse. He kills his own for sport."

Grozz's Offer

He'll tell the party exactly where Bitter Breath is and how many guards are in each room. He'll even unlock the drainage gate. In return: let him leave with 5 of his soldiers. They'll desert into the wasteland.

  • DC 12 Insight: He's genuinely terrified. This is real.
  • If they let him go: −6 hobgoblins from the final fight. Grozz may return as a future ally.
  • If they kill him: Quick, quiet, efficient. But Drenwal's god might have something to say about executing a surrendering prisoner.
  • If they take him prisoner: Slows them down. He'll cooperate but weighs on resources.
Gargauth (if they kill Grozz)

"See? Sometimes mercy is just weakness with a prettier name."

Short Rest
10 minutes real time

If the party took fights in the infiltration, they'll want a short rest before the boss. Let them have it — Warlock needs slots, Rogue needs hit dice, Cleric may need spell slots.

Moonkite's Aura Bonus
Anyone who short rests within 30 ft of the Moonkite's cage regains +1 hit die (the Moonkite's aura, even caged, is restorative).
Aurora Hears the Moonkite
Not words. A melody — like wind through crystal. Sad, exhausted, but aware. It knows someone is here. It knows Aurora is here. The Wand of Celestial Warding glows steadily — not pulsing anymore. Matching the rhythm of the cage's silver light. Connected.
Drenwal, During the Rest
A flash of vision: Dumal, miles away, standing at the edge of the river of blood. Looking south. Looking toward the Palace. He felt it when the party arrived — Bhaal's children can sense each other at proximity. He's coming. Not today. But soon.
Bhaal Influence
Tick to 4/6 (proximity to death, violence, and the impulse to kill Bitter Breath rather than negotiate).
Act 4: Bitter Breath Boss Fight
Estimated time: 1.5 hours
Setup

Bitter Breath is in the Throne Room when the party arrives (or emerges from the lower level). If the party freed Grozz, reduce the hobgoblin count. If they were stealthy, they may get a surprise round.

Encounter Composition
Creature Count HP AC Role
Bitter Breath 1 280 19 Boss — mobile skirmisher
Hobgoblin Veterans 4 (or 2 if Grozz freed) 55 18 Front line, phalanx
Barbed Devil 1 110 15 Ranged harassment (Hurl Flame)
Total CR Budget
~20–22 (Deadly for 3 level 11 PCs — this is the climax)
Lair Actions (Initiative 20)

The Palace of Gore is Bitter Breath's domain. Pick one each round:

1. Gore Geyser

A geyser of blood erupts from the floor under one creature. DC 16 Dex save or 3d8 necrotic damage and knocked prone. The blood is difficult terrain (10-ft radius) until initiative 20 next round.

2. Bone Cage

Bones erupt from the walls and form a cage around one creature. DC 16 Str save or restrained until the end of their next turn. The cage has AC 12 and 25 HP and can be destroyed by allies.

3. Bloodbath's Echo

The throne pulses with residual energy. All creatures within 30 ft of the throne must make a DC 15 WIS save or be frightened of the throne (not Bitter Breath) until initiative 20 next round. Bitter Breath is immune.

Bitter Breath's Tactics
Round 1
  • Opens with Rotten Breath (40-ft cone, DC 17 Con, 8d8 poison + 4d6 necrotic, stun on fail by 5+)
  • Targets the cluster — ideally hitting 2+ PCs
  • Uses Skirmisher (bonus action Dash) to reposition behind cover or hobgoblins
Rounds 2–3
  • Multiattack with Blood Frenzy advantage on wounded PCs
  • Legendary Actions: Reposition to avoid Asimov's Sneak Attack setup, Gutripper Strike on Drenwal to break Spirit Guardians concentration
  • Hobgoblins form shield wall to body-block Asimov
At Half HP (140)
  • Gets MORE aggressive, not less
  • Starts using Flaying Rush every round with legendary actions — dashing through the party, slashing everyone
Bitter Breath (Half HP)

"My brother fell to you. MY BROTHER. And you think YOU can take ME?"

"I am NOT him. I am BETTER."

Relentless Triggers (0 HP → 1 HP)

Bitter Breath drops, then stands back up. Dead silence from him. Then:

Bitter Breath (Relentless)

"...No."

He makes one final Rotten Breath (if recharged) or a full Multiattack with reckless abandon.

Anti-Party Tactics
vs Asimov (Rogue)

Hobgoblin phalanx denies easy flanking. Bitter Breath uses Reposition to stay away from Asimov. If Asimov gets Sneak Attack consistently, have Bitter Breath use Evasive Gore to halve and reposition on reaction. Bone Cage lair action to restrain him.

vs Aurora (Warlock)

Barbed Devil focuses Hurl Flame (4d6 fire, 150 ft) on Aurora to force concentration checks. Bitter Breath may use Flaying Rush to pass through Aurora's position.

vs Drenwal (Cleric)

Aura of Rot (DC 17 Con or poisoned + half speed) punishes his frontline position. Gutripper Strike legendary action on him to force concentration checks on Spirit Guardians. Lair action Bone Cage to lock him down and cut off healing.

Bitter Breath's Dialogue (Mid-Fight)
Bitter Breath

"My brother was a drunk and a glutton. He sat on that throne and gorged himself. I EARNED this."

"You fed him to a genie? You didn't even have the dignity to kill him properly."

Bitter Breath (to Aurora, if she heads toward the prison)

"Going somewhere? That thing in the cage — it's MINE. Its light powers my fortress. Without it, these walls crumble. You think I'll let you take it?"

Bitter Breath (to Drenwal)

"I can smell it on you. The blood god's stink. You're no better than the demons I command."

Victory Conditions

Bitter Breath dies: The hobgoblins surrender or flee. The barbed devil teleports away (it has no loyalty). The Palace is the party's — for now.

The party retreats: Bitter Breath does NOT pursue far. He's territorial. But he'll double the guards and the Moonkite rescue becomes much harder next time.

Act 5: The Moonkite
Estimated time: 30–45 minutes
Reaching the Cage

After the fight, the party descends to the Prison Level. The chain devil guarding the cage either:

  • Attacks (if it hasn't been dealt with)
  • Flees (if it senses its master is dead — DC 14 Intimidation or it fights)
  • Is already dead (if the party cleared this level during infiltration)
The Cage
The cage is a sphere of hellforged adamantine, 10 feet across, suspended by chains that run into the walls and ceiling. Each chain pulses with sickly orange light — they're siphoning energy. Inside the cage, a shape floats.
It's hard to look at directly. Silver-white light, folded wings, something between a stag and a crane and a comet. It's smaller than it should be — diminished, drained. Its light flickers like a candle in a storm.
The Wand of Celestial Warding in Aurora's hand blazes. For the first time, it speaks — not in words, but in knowing. Aurora understands, with absolute certainty: this is the Moonkite. This is what she's been connected to since before she was born. And it's dying.
Using the Key — Skill Challenge

Aurora uses the Moonkite Cage Key. The cage clicks, hisses, and the adamantine panels slide apart. The chains resist — they're still siphoning.

Skill Challenge: Breaking the Chains
3 successes before 2 failures
Check DC Description
Athletics/Str 18 Physically tear a chain from the wall
Arcana 16 Identify the siphoning enchantment and disrupt it
Religion 15 Channel divine energy to purify a chain (Drenwal's specialty)
Thieves' Tools 17 Pick the chain anchors (Asimov's specialty)
Wand of Celestial Warding 14 Aurora channels the wand's energy to resonate with the Moonkite and weaken the chains

On 3 successes: The chains shatter. Silver light floods the room. The Moonkite unfurls.

On 2 failures: The chains tighten. The Moonkite screams (psychic wave — DC 14 WIS save or stunned 1 round). The party can try again but the DCs increase by 2. The Palace begins to shake — Bitter Breath wasn't lying; the Moonkite's energy was holding the structure together.

The Moonkite Freed
The chains shatter like glass. Silver light — real light, not Avernus fire — floods the chamber, then the hallway, then erupts from the Palace roof like a beacon. For one moment, the sky above the Palace of Gore shows stars. Real stars. Not the burning hellscape — a window to another sky.
The Moonkite unfolds. It's larger than the cage should have allowed — wings spanning 20 feet, a body of living starlight, antlers that branch like frozen lightning. It touches down before Aurora. Its eyes — vast, ancient, gentle — meet hers.
What Aurora Knows (No Words — Communicated Through Feeling)
  • Gratitude. Deep, endless.
  • Weakness. It was drained for decades. It cannot fight. It cannot fly far. It needs time.
  • Warning. The chains were connected to something larger. Zariel's war machine network. Freeing the Moonkite has sent a signal. They will come.
  • Gift. The Moonkite touches the Wand of Celestial Warding with one antler. The wand evolves.
Wand of Celestial Warding — Evolution
Level 2 Abilities (Immediate)
  • Charges increase to 7 (regains 1d4+3 at dawn)
  • New spell: Shield (1 charge)
  • New spell: Counterspell at 3rd level (3 charges)
  • Moonkite's Blessing: Once per long rest, Aurora can cast Banishment (no concentration, 1 minute duration) using the wand. The banished creature is sent to the Feywild rather than its home plane.
Consequences & Cliffhangers
Immediate
  • The Palace begins to crumble without the Moonkite's energy holding it together. The party has 10 minutes to loot and leave before structural collapse.
Quick Loot from Bitter Breath's Quarters
  • 3 soul coins
  • Bitter Breath's Gutripper — +1 shortsword, 2d6 slashing + 1d6 necrotic, on crit target bleeds 1d6/round
  • A crude map of Avernus showing the locations of several Blood Pay sites (marked with Xs — Bitter Breath was planning raids)
The Signal

Freeing the Moonkite sent a pulse of celestial energy across Avernus. Multiple parties detected it:

  • Zariel's forces — an ice devil patrol will be dispatched (future encounter)
  • Dumal — felt the pulse. Knows EXACTLY where the party is now. He's 12–18 hours away.
  • Smiler (if they dealt with him) — racing to claim the Palace before it collapses
The Moonkite's Refuge
  • The Moonkite cannot travel fast. It will follow the party in the Feywild echo — invisible to most, but Aurora can feel its presence.
  • For now: Aurora can communicate with the Moonkite once per long rest (brief visions, feelings, direction-sense). This replaces the vague wand pulses with actual guidance.
Drenwal's Cliffhanger

As they leave the collapsing Palace, Drenwal sees it. Carved into the stone above the exit, fresh — the stone is still warm:

A symbol of Bhaal. And below it, in Infernal:

"I SEE YOU, BROTHER."
DM Note
Dumal was here. While they were inside. He watched them enter, and chose not to attack. He's waiting.

End of Session.
