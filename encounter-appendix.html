The Smog of the Damned

Environmental hazard active during the approach to the Palace of Gore. The gore river generates a choking miasma of sulphurous vapor, rotting blood fumes, and infernal ash. It gets worse the closer the party gets.

Phase 1 — The Creeping Haze (first hour)
  • Lightly obscured. Disadvantage on Perception beyond 60 ft.
  • DC 12 CON save every 30 min or gain 1 level of exhaustion.
  • Ship crew makes same saves. Crew failure = -10 ft ship speed per failed member.
Phase 2 — The Blood Fog (hours 2-3)
  • Heavily obscured beyond 30 ft. Attack rolls beyond 30 ft have disadvantage.
  • DC 14 CON save every 20 min or gain 1 exhaustion.
  • -3 penalty to all Survival/Perception navigation and scouting checks.
  • Tracking the Palace: DC 17 Survival (normally DC 14).
  • Non-magical light sources dim to half radius. Magical light draws attention (DC 14 Stealth or patrols notice).
Phase 3 — The Choking Pall (within 1 mile of Palace)
  • Heavily obscured beyond 15 ft. The smog is thick enough to taste.
  • DC 15 CON save every 10 min or gain 1 exhaustion AND take 1d6 poison damage.
  • Crew refuses to continue at 2+ exhaustion. Party must proceed on foot.
  • Moonbow dilemma: Aurora's Moonbow creates 10-ft clean air radius (no saves). But the silver light makes stealth impossible. Choose: breathe or hide.
Mitigation: Protection from Poison (1 creature, 1h). Gust of Wind (clears 60-ft line, 1 round). Wet cloth (advantage on CON saves). Salve of Shadow (immunity to poison damage, 1h). Smiler's drainage tunnel bypasses Phase 3.
