# Encounter Tables & Combat Reference

## Party Profile: 3 Strong Level 11 PCs

| PC | Class | Role | Key Abilities |
|----|-------|------|---------------|
| Drenwal | Soulknife Rogue | Burst DPS, stealth, infiltration | Psychic Blades + Sneak Attack (6d6), Psychic Whispers, expertise skills, Uncanny Dodge, Evasion |
| Aurora | Warlock | Sustained ranged DPS, utility | Eldritch Blast (3 beams), limited but potent spell slots (5th level), Wand of Celestial Warding |
| Cleric | Cleric (TBD) | Healer, support, frontline | Spirit Guardians, Spiritual Weapon, healing, condition removal, 6th-level slots, Destroy Undead |

### Scaling Philosophy

This party punches above its weight. Three-player parties are normally weaker, but this composition covers all bases — burst, sustain, healing, stealth, social. Key vulnerabilities to exploit:

- **Action economy:** Only 3 PCs. Fights with 1 big enemy are too easy (Rogue shreds single targets). Add **4-6 minions** or **legendary/lair actions** to every boss.
- **Concentration pressure:** Spirit Guardians and Hex/other Warlock concentration spells are force multipliers. Break concentration with multi-hit attacks, forced movement, or environmental damage.
- **Resource drain:** Warlock has few slots, Cleric burns through spells fast healing. Chain encounters without long rests.
- **Psychic resistance:** Soulknife deals psychic damage. Devils with psychic resistance or immunity (rare but impactful) force Drenwal to adapt.
- **AoE weakness:** No dedicated blaster. Swarm tactics with spread-out enemies are harder for this party than clustered groups.
- **Saving throws:** Target Str and Int saves — likely the party's weakest. Rogue has Evasion for Dex saves. Cleric covers Wis saves.

## Warlord Encounter Profiles

### Bloodbath the Drinker of Gore (CR 15-16)
- **Forces:** Bloodbath + 2 barbed devil bodyguards (CR 5, 110 HP each) + 1d4 bearded devil fodder
- **Environmental hazard:** Blood pool (difficult terrain, DC 15 Con or blinded by gore spray)
- **Behavior:** Tests strength. Respects worthy opponents. Focuses the Cleric first (wants to see if they fight or just heal).
- **At half HP:** Sacrifices a bodyguard to heal 50 HP, triggers Aura of Gore (DC 17 Con or frightened, 30 ft)
- **Anti-Rogue:** Truesight 30 ft. Can't be sneak attacked from hiding easily.
- **Dialogue:** Speaks of blood, strength, glory
- **Reward if impressed:** Infernal weapons, soul coins, information, passage through his territory

### Smiler the Defiler (Recurring)
- **Behavior:** Ambush, taunt, flee. Leaves traps (glyphs of warding, explosive barrels).
- **Cannot be pinned down.** Escapes on war machine. Has Counterspell to block Hold Person/etc.
- **Useful as:** Information conduit when party is stuck
- **Bitter Rivals adventure:** Asks party to help fight Bitter Breath's hobgoblins
- **Anti-party trick:** Targets Aurora with Silence to shut down Eldritch Blast verbal component + Cleric spells

### Princeps Kovic (CR 13-15)
- **Forces:** Kovic + 6 merregons (AC 17, 45 HP, phalanx formation) + 2 barbed devil snipers + 1 war machine with ballista
- **Behavior:** Capture, not kill. Disciplined formation tactics. Shield wall forces melee engagement.
- **Anti-Rogue:** Merregon phalanx denies flanking. Nets (DC 15 Str/Dex to escape) to pin Drenwal.
- **DC 17 Cha** to talk down. Can become uneasy ally.
- **Wants:** Adamantine rods, prisoners, intelligence

### Rust Queen
- **Forces:** 3 rust monsters (target Cleric's metal armor/shield) + 2 meazels + 2 barbed devils + corrosive fog
- **Behavior:** Acid pools (2d6 acid/round), corrosive fog (DC 16 Con or disadvantage on attacks), weapon degradation
- **Anti-Cleric:** Rust monsters specifically target the Cleric's metal equipment. Force tough choices about gear.
- **DC 17 Dex** to avoid war machine accidents in her territory

## Scaled Encounter Templates (3 Strong Level 11 PCs)

### Easy: Travel Tax (CR 8-10)
*Goal: drain a spell slot or two, no real danger*
- 6 hell hounds (CR 3, 45 HP each) — pack tactics, surround
- OR 1 bone devil (CR 9, 142 HP) + 4 lemures (fodder, absorb attacks)
- End in 2-3 rounds. No legendary actions needed.

### Medium: Standard Combat (CR 12-14)
*Goal: meaningful resource drain, someone might go down briefly*
- 1 chain devil (CR 8, 85 HP) + 2 barbed devils (CR 5, 110 HP) + 4 spined devils (CR 2, 22 HP, flying ranged harassment)
- Add barbed devil reinforcement at round 3 if party is cruising
- **Environmental:** Lava cracks (2d10 fire if pushed/moved into), toxic fumes (DC 14 Con or poisoned)
- **Anti-Rogue note:** Spined devils fly and shoot from 100 ft — hard for Drenwal to Sneak Attack without creative play

### Hard: Elite Combat (CR 15-18)
*Goal: real danger, force resource expenditure, tactical challenge*
- 1 narzugon (CR 13, 112 HP) + 2 barbed devils (CR 5, 110 HP) + 1 nightmare mount (CR 3, Ethereal Stride escape)
- Boss gets **2 legendary actions:** Lance Attack (1), Terrifying Command (1)
- **Environmental hazard:** Collapsing structure (initiative 20, random 10-ft square, DC 16 Dex or 4d10 bludgeoning), or war machine fuel leak (10-ft radius, any fire damage = 6d6 fire explosion)
- **Anti-Cleric:** Narzugon's Terrifying Command forces Wis save — if Cleric fails, they can't approach to heal or use Spirit Guardians effectively
- **Anti-Warlock:** Barbed devils Hurl Flame to force concentration checks on Aurora

### Deadly: Boss Fight (CR 19-22+)
*Goal: climactic, memorable, possible PC death*
- 1 death knight (CR 17, 180 HP, 19th-level paladin spells) + 2 wights (CR 3, drain life) + 6 skeletons (fodder, action economy)
- OR 1 ice devil (CR 14, 180 HP) + 1 bone devil (CR 9, 142 HP) + 4 merregons (shield wall, body-block the Rogue)
- Boss gets **3 legendary actions** and **lair actions** (initiative 20)
- **Lair ideas:** Chains erupt from ground (restrain, DC 16 Str), hellfire vents (3d6 fire, 10-ft radius), summoning circle pulses (1d4 lemures appear)
- **Win condition alternative:** Offer a non-combat out (devil's bargain, surrender terms, environmental solution) to reward creative play

### Moral Dilemma Combat
*Goal: combat + tough choice simultaneously*
- Present civilians, prisoners, or soul coins mid-fight
- "The devil offers to stop fighting if you hand over 3 soul coins and Lulu"
- "The prisoners will die in 3 rounds unless someone breaks the chains (DC 16 Athletics, full action)"
- "The soul coin powering the enemy war machine contains someone the Cleric recognizes"
- **Design note:** These are devastating for 3-player parties because pulling one PC out of combat to solve the dilemma cuts your damage by 33%

## Anti-Party Tactics Reference

Use these sparingly — not every fight, but mix them in to keep players on their toes.

| Tactic | Targets | Effect |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Grapple/restrain the Rogue | Drenwal | No Sneak Attack without advantage, can't disengage freely |
| Silence spell/effect | Aurora + Cleric | Shuts down Eldritch Blast (verbal), most Cleric spells |
| Forced movement (push/pull) | Cleric | Pull them out of Spirit Guardians range of allies, or push allies out |
| Multi-hit attacks | Aurora/Cleric | Multiple Con saves to break concentration |
| Darkness/fog | All | Negates Sneak Attack (can't see target), negates ranged |
| Counterspell | Cleric | Block clutch heals or Spirit Guardians. Arcanaloths have this. |
| Swarm/surround | All | Spread damage, prevent Rogue from isolating targets |
| Flying enemies | Drenwal | Soulknife is melee-focused, can't reach flyers easily (60-ft thrown range helps but no Sneak Attack without ally adjacent) |

## Combat Pacing Guide (3-Player Sessions)

- **Max 2 combat encounters per session** (3-player combats take longer with fewer actions)
- **1 Hard + 1 Easy** or **1 Deadly** is a good session rhythm
- **Short rest after every hard fight** — Warlock needs this for slots
- **Long rest scarcity** is your difficulty lever. Avernus doesn't offer safe rests easily.
- **Round target:** Boss fights should last 4-6 rounds. If ending in 2-3, the boss was too weak.
- **Retreat option:** Always have an escape route for enemies. Devils are smart — they flee, regroup, ambush later. This creates recurring antagonists.
