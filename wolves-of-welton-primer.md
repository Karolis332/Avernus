# Wolves of Welton — Oneshot Primer

**System:** D&D 5E
**Tier:** 2nd–3rd level (4–5 PCs)
**Run time:** 3.5–4 hours
**Source:** Winghorn Press, *The Wolves of Welton*
**Genre tag:** Pastoral mystery → moral-choice combat

---

## DESIGN THESIS

> The hook is "kill the wolves." The truth is "the wolves are people now."

This oneshot lives or dies on **one reveal**: the wolves talk. Everything before it is sheep-rustling investigation; everything after it is a negotiated peace under threat of a civil war inside the wolf pack itself.

Three things make it work at the table:

1. **The bounty is suspiciously generous** (800 gp for a wolf cull). Hint it loud. Suspicious players investigate; greedy players still get the moral reveal in the cave.
2. **Featherock is the keystone clue.** A halfling shepherd, broken, whispering "the wolves were *talking*." If players skip him, the cave reveal lands cold. If they believe him, they walk in with their swords lowered.
3. **Flame vs Bolt is the real boss fight.** Two alpha wolves, one wants peace, one wants slaughter. The party picks which one wins by who they kill, who they spare, and who they talk to.

**Win state:** Wolves dealt with (peace or kill) **AND** the council pays **AND** the party can live with what they did. Lying to the council is on the table — Father Merriksonn rolls Insight +5 against Deception.

---

## CAMPAIGN-LEVEL BACKGROUND (DM ONLY)

Three months ago, the village sorcerer **Alexi Merriksonn** — wielder of Wild Magic — was patrolling the moors and stumbled into a normal wolf pack. He tried to drive them off, **rolled a Wild Magic Surge**, and was vaporized. The surge dumped his intelligence, language, and a sliver of his elemental power into the surviving wolves.

The pack woke up sapient. They learned Common in days. They built a fence. They drew on cave walls. They drove off the local Owlbear and took its den. They stole sheep — not to slaughter, but to **breed**. They're trying to live.

The alpha pair, **Flame** and **Bolt**, got the most magic — fire breath and lightning breath, respectively. They disagree on what to do next:

- **Flame** (chaotic neutral): kill humans, terrorize them out of the woods, claim the land.
- **Bolt** (lawful neutral): negotiate or retreat, raise pups, never be hunted again.

Within weeks they will kill each other if the party doesn't intervene.

**Nobody in Welton knows any of this.** The village thinks it's a normal pack acting weird.

## COLD OPEN

Read aloud:

> The road from the high passes drops down through chalk hills and patchy woods, and below you sits Welton — white-walled houses, smoke from a dozen chimneys, sheep dotting the green like spilled flour. Easy country. Quiet country.
>
> Three hundred feet ahead, the road bends between a rock outcrop and the edge of a mossy wood. Two men in shepherd's cloaks are pushing a small flock through the gap, four sheepdogs working the flanks.
>
> Then the dogs go rigid. Ears up. Both of them barking at once. The shepherds reach for clubs at their belts — and from both sides of the road, grey shapes erupt out of the brush. Eight wolves. Moving like a single thing.

Roll initiative. Players are at **150 ft** distance from the action.

## SCENE 1 — THE WELTON WOLFPACK

**Encounter:** 8 Wolves (MM p.241) vs. 2 Shepherds + 4 Sheepdogs + flock of sheep.

### Wolf Tactics (Critical)

The wolves are **NOT** trying to kill. They are stealing sheep.

- **4 wolves** harass shepherds — bite at legs/arms, do NOT go for kill blows. Goal: distraction.
- **4 wolves** grab sheep by the scruff and bolt west into the woods at full speed (50 ft/round).
- If a wolf takes >50% HP damage: it disengages and runs.
- If 3 wolves are dropped: the rest break contact and flee west, leaving stolen sheep if necessary.
- **No wolf fights to the death here.** This is a smash-and-grab.

### Shepherd Stats (if needed)

Use **Commoner** (MM p.345) — AC 10, HP 4, club +2 (1d4 bludgeoning). Sheepdogs use **Mastiff** (MM p.332).

### Aftermath

If the party intervened: shepherds (**Hennick** and **Toll**, both farmhands) are grateful, give first round of **Shaggy Wolf Stories** (see Rumor Table) on the walk back. **No reward** — they're poor.

If the party watched: shepherds lose 6–8 sheep, are wounded, beg help to walk back to Welton. No hostility, just exhaustion.

**XP:** 400 (8 × 50). Award full XP even if wolves fled.

## SCENE 2 — WELTON & THE COUNCIL

### Welton at a Glance

- Population ~300. Human-majority, halfling minority, handful of dwarves.
- Economy: wheat + wool. Exports both to nearby cities.
- Mood: **scared, hungry, idle.** Farmers driven off their land are camping in the village. Streets are muddy with extra foot traffic.
- Buildings: bright-white painted oak, carved eaves. Slow river. Two hills flanking.

### Locations

| Location | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **The Shepherd's Crook** (inn) | Council meeting, Featherock's sickroom, free lodging if hired |
| **Growers' Hall** | Tillus Merrion's HQ (across from inn) |
| **Fleecers' Hall** | Wool guild (across from inn) |
| **General store** | Basic gear, nothing exciting |
| **Father Merriksonn's chapel** | Small shrine to Pelor, mostly empty |

### The Shepherd's Crook

Owner: **Leanor Slatebeard** (dwarf, ~110 yrs old). Her husband **Banteth** cooks and brews. Free round + meal if the party identifies as adventurers there for the wolves. Will guide them upstairs to Featherock if asked.

Common room mood: old shepherds in cloaks smoking long pipes, muttering. Younger displaced farmers nursing one drink at a time. Dour, suspicious of outsiders until the wolf job is mentioned.

### The Council Meeting (back room, when party arrives)

**8-seat round table. 7 chairs filled.**

| Councillor | Role | Voice |
|---|---|---|
| **Tillus Merrion** (halfling, m) | De facto mayor, Growers & Buyers Assoc. leader | Blustery, ledger-obsessed, wants exports to continue |
| **Father Johan Merriksonn** (human, m, ~55) | Priest of Pelor, moral compass | Wants to feed locals over honoring contracts |
| **Corel** (human, m, ~50) | Fleecers' Guild, master shepherd | Quiet, respected. Tuft of wool tied to his vest |
| **Hanna Westly** (human, f) | Farmer rep | Worried, mostly silent |
| **3 other human men** | Minor guild reps | Background voices |
| **Mella Tinnsfoot** (halfling, f) | Halfling community rep | Sides with Tillus by default |

When the party enters, **Tillus and Father Merriksonn are mid-shouting-match** over whether to halt grain exports during the crisis.

### What the Council Offers

- **800 gp** total reward, paid on completion
- **Free lodging** at the Shepherd's Crook
- **Free meals** during the contract
- **A local guide** (Corel) if requested

### What the Council Knows

- Wolves driving people off farms for **3 months**
- Whole flocks of sheep, plus cattle, pigs, chickens going missing
- Wolves act **strangely organized** — opening gates, pulling down fences, ambushing shepherds on the move
- A posse of village men was attacked in the woods and routed weeks ago. Survivors talk of "thunder" and "fire."
- The village sorcerer, **Alexi Merriksonn** (Father Merriksonn's brother), vanished around the same time the attacks started
- Wolves are believed to den in the western woods
- **Werewolves?** Father Merriksonn shoots this down — full moons have passed, nobody turned

### Featherock's Room (Upstairs at the Inn)

**Willen Featherock**, halfling shepherd, badly wounded weeks ago in the failed posse. White-faced, sweating, bandaged left arm and right leg. Father Merriksonn attends him daily.

Featherock is **scared** and won't talk freely. He will open up if the party **promises to believe him**.

**What he says:**
- He was bitten, then trampled by his fleeing companions during the posse ambush
- He drifted in and out of consciousness
- He remembers **gruff voices arguing in the darkness** and a **powerful grip on his ankle**
- If pressed gently: *"They were talking. The wolves were talking. They were arguing whether to eat me."*
- He was dumped on the Westly farm doorstep at dawn — **alive**, deliberately
- After this he breaks down crying. Won't say more.

**This is the keystone clue.** If players believe him, they walk into Scene 5 ready to parley. If they dismiss him as cracked, the den fight escalates faster.

## SCENE 3 — INTO THE WESTERN WOODS

The wolves' den is in a **hollow on the far western edge of the woods**, ~4 hours' walking from Welton.

**There is no scripted path.** Let players choose:

| Approach | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Track from Scene 1 ambush site** | Wisdom (Survival) DC 14 (DC 16 if >8h old). Failure costs 1 hour and a random encounter. |
| **Bait with sheep flock (Corel offers his)** | 6 wolves attack in 2–4 hours. Same tactics as Scene 1. If party speaks, wolves may say a word or two then flee in confusion. |
| **Hire Corel as guide** | Commoner stats, WIS 14, Survival +4. Cuts travel to 3 hours, no tracking checks needed. He will NOT enter the final hollow with them. |
| **Magic / Divination** | DM call. Speak with Animals on a captured wolf yields fragments: "Pack," "Lightbringer," "no kill shepherd." |

### Travel Skill Check Table

| Check | DC | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Track wolf through woods | WIS (Survival) 14 (16 if old) | |
| Track wolf carrying prey | WIS (Survival) 11 (13 if old) | Blood trail, drag marks |
| Hide from wolves | DEX (Stealth) vs Wolf Perception +3 (advantage on scent/sound) | |
| Understand wolf habits / behavior | WIS (Nature) 12 | DC 10 with proficiency |
| Spot the cave's tripwire perimeter | WIS (Perception) 13 | Triggers bone noisemakers |

### Atmosphere (read on travel)

> The woods press in tight. Moss-thick trunks, knee-high ferns, the smell of wet earth. Every few minutes something small bolts through the undergrowth — squirrels, rabbits, you can't tell. Once, far off to the west, a single wolf howls. Then nothing. Just the rustle.

## SCENE 4 — THE OWLBEAR (OPTIONAL)

**Cut this scene if you're tight on time.** It exists for combat depth and to foreshadow that *wolves drove an Owlbear out of its den.*

After ~2 hours in the woods, the party hears **snapping branches**. Unless they hide (DEX Stealth DC 15), an **Owlbear** (MM p.249) crashes into them.

### Modifications

- **Max HP reduced to 40** (was 59) — it's been bleeding for weeks
- Limping, gaunt, half-healed bite wounds clotted with old black blood
- **Scorch marks** in the fur (Flame's work)
- **It will not retreat.** Starving. Charges the easiest target. Fights to 0 HP.

### Clues After the Kill

- WIS (Nature) DC 12: wounds are 2+ weeks old, caused by **sharp jaws** consistent with wolves
- WIS (Nature) DC 10 OR proficiency: **wolves do not pick fights with owlbears**, this is unnatural
- If daytime: owlbears are nocturnal. **It was driven from its lair.**
- Scorch marks: WIS (Survival) DC 12 — natural fire didn't do this. Magical heat.

**XP:** 700 (Owlbear at CR 3, but reduced HP)

## SCENE 5 — THE WOLVES' DEN

### Map Overview

A **cave entrance** ~10 ft wide × 10 ft tall in a low grey cliff, opening into a hollow at the western edge of the woods. Smoke rises from a **second hole ~30 ft back from the entrance, on the hill above** (chimney to the eastern sub-chamber).

**Tripwire perimeter** at the treeline (WIS Perception DC 13). Triggers bone noisemakers — alerts everything.

### What the Party Sees from the Treeline

- **6 wolves** milling around the hollow
- Some lounging by an outdoor fire
- Some **working on a wooden fence** with their teeth
- Some tearing apart a sheep carcass
- **Many wear strips of colored cloth, beadwork, or simple ornaments** around limbs or necks
- The scene is **eerily peaceful and organized**. Not a wolf den. A *settlement.*

**The reveal moment.** Pause. Let the players notice.

### The Back Door (Chimney Hole)

The smoke hole on the hill is **30 ft from the cave entrance**, opens 15 ft above the floor of the **eastern sub-chamber** directly above the meeting-room fire. Big enough for a Small creature (halfling, gnome) to squeeze through, or a Medium creature with grappling and effort.

**Eavesdroppers here will hear Flame and Bolt arguing.** Set the tone:

**Flame** (snarled, hot): *"They came armed. They came as hunters. We are weak as long as they think we are animals. Burn the next village. Make them flee. Take the land."*

**Bolt** (low, thunder-soft): *"And every season after, more hunters. More fire-throwers like the Lightbringer. We did not become **this** to die in a glorious last stand. We disappear. We go deep. The pups grow up free."*

**Flame:** *"The pups grow up **starving** if we crawl."*

(Continue ad-lib until the party acts. The argument lasts 5–10 minutes in-fiction.)

### Inside the Cave — Four Chambers

**Central chamber (largest):**
- Pack's main living/sleeping area
- Piles of bones in southeast corner
- Crude leaf-and-cloth beds
- **Cave paintings** in charcoal and white ash on the walls — hunting wolves, a tall human figure throwing light, the human vanishing in a burst, then the wolves rising
- WIS (Perception) DC 12 to read the story in the paintings

**Western sub-chamber — Storeroom:**
- Bags of homespun wool, leather hides
- A couple knives, a shovel, mundane stolen gear
- Stolen sheep penned behind a **crude wooden fence** (some branches show tool-shaping marks)
- Constant bleating

**Central sub-chamber — Nursery:**
- ~6 wolf pups, just learning Common ("hunt?", "no", "mama")
- **2 adult wolf guards** — will die for the pups
- Use **Cat** stats (MM p.320) for pups in combat

**Eastern sub-chamber — Council Room:**
- Small fire (chimney to surface)
- Otherwise bare
- Where Flame and Bolt argue

### Triggering Combat

The wolves panic-bark if:
- Noisemakers trip
- Stealth checks fail
- Party walks into the hollow openly

One wolf shouts **"HUNTERS!"** in growling, halting Common.

**Unless the party defuses immediately**, all 6 outer wolves attack. After **1 full round**, Flame and Bolt emerge from the cave with **4 more armored wolves** (+1 AC from crude leather scraps).

### Combat Tactics (Read This Before Running)

- **All wolves are intelligent.** They use Pack Tactics aggressively.
- They use speed (50 ft) to **outflank squishy casters in the back lines.**
- **Bolt** uses Lightning Breath in lines that **never catch allies.**
- **Flame** uses Fire Breath in cones and **does not care if one or two wolves get caught** — she'll trade them for a full party blast.
- Wolves prone targets with bite + DC 13 STR save, then dogpile.

### THE PEACE TRIGGER

After **ANY** of these three things happens, **Bolt roars** (thunder-shake the room) and bellows in Common:

> "**HOLD!** This fight serves none of us. Speak — or we all die for nothing."

Triggers (any one):
- 4 wolves are killed
- Flame OR Bolt drops below half HP
- Any PC drops to 0 HP

The party can ignore him. If they do, the wolves fight to the death. If they listen, **roll initiative for peace** — the talk scene begins. See Scene 6.

## SCENE 6 — SETTLING THE SCORES

### Branch A — Total War (Party Refuses or Fails Peace)

Wolves fight to the death. Most die. Survivors:

- **The nursery wolves** beg for their pups' lives. Two desperate adults guarding ~6 pups. If pups are threatened, the adults **fight to the death** screaming for the pups to flee. If the pups are spared, the surviving wolves vanish into the wilderness, swearing never to return.
- **The contract is fulfilled.** Party returns to Welton. Council pays 800 gp.
- **Featherock does not recover.** He'll live, but the haunted look stays. Never the same.

### Branch B — Negotiated Peace (Bolt Lives, Talks)

If the party stands down (Scene 5 Peace Trigger), **Bolt sits down and explains everything:**

- The pack was normal until they brought down "the Lightbringer" (Alexi Merriksonn) ~3 months ago
- A flash of bright light. They woke up *thinking.*
- They've been hoarding food so pups don't starve in winter and so the elderly don't get left behind
- They drove off the Owlbear that had plagued the woods for years
- **He hates Welton's people** (centuries of indiscriminate killing of wolves) but **he is not Flame** — he wants out, not war
- His offer: the woods + uninhabited western moors belong to the pack. Pack does not raid. Pack kills any other predators that threaten Welton. **A border.**

### THE FLAME BETRAYAL (Critical Moment)

The instant it becomes clear Bolt is striking a deal, **Flame attacks Bolt from behind.**

- She bites him for **massive damage** (DM call — drop him to ~1/4 HP, take him out of the fight)
- She screams "**COWARD!**" in Common, eyes burning
- All **armored wolves** rally to her in a final, desperate attack on the party
- Unarmored wolves **fall back in fear and confusion** — they don't know who to follow

**This is the real boss fight.** Flame + 4 armored wolves vs the party, with Bolt bleeding on the cave floor.

If the party wins **AND** stabilizes Bolt (DC 10 WIS Medicine, or any healing): peace holds. Bolt becomes the new alpha. The pack is salvageable.

If Bolt dies in the chaos: peace dies with him. Surviving wolves flee. Long-term, **a wounded, leaderless intelligent wolf pack vanishes into the wild** — DM seed for a future adventure.

### Returning to Welton

**Option 1 — Total Kill, Truthful Report.** Council pays 800 gp on the spot. No complications.

**Option 2 — Total Kill, Lied About Pups/Negotiation.** No one to lie to. Same as Option 1.

**Option 3 — Wolves Relocated/Negotiated, Lie About It.** Party says "wiped them out." Father Merriksonn rolls **WIS (Insight) +5** opposed by **CHA (Deception)** check. Use the **lowest** party Deception roll. If he beats it, he confronts them in **private** — not in front of the council. He's heartbroken (his brother died for this) but will not expose the lie if they explain. He still pays.

**Option 4 — Wolves Relocated/Negotiated, Honest Pitch.** Party brings the deal to the council.
  - **Father Merriksonn** and **Corel** support the deal automatically (Merriksonn because his brother's legacy is intelligence given to wolves; Corel because he respects sapient creatures regardless of form).
  - **Tillus Merrion** must be convinced. **DC 15 CHA (Persuasion)** + a "decent argument" (DM judgment). If Tillus folds, the council unanimously agrees.
  - **If Tillus refuses:** Corel calls a vote on the council floor. Party makes **DC 12 CHA (Persuasion)** against each of the 5 remaining council members (3 humans + Hanna Westly + Mella Tinnsfoot). Advantage/disadvantage based on argument quality. **Need 3 successes** to win majority (Corel and Merriksonn vote yes automatically).
  - **If outvoted:** Tillus storms out broken, leaves the council, never returns. The pack relocates anyway.
  - **Either way, 800 gp paid.**

### Featherock's Recovery

If the wolves' intelligence becomes **publicly known**, Featherock begins recovering rapidly within days. The man knew he wasn't crazy, and being believed heals him. He'll come find the party in person to thank them before they leave the village.

**Tillus Merrion** — Halfling, m. Pompous mayor. Loves Welton, treats lives as ledger entries. Bluster, then folds under good argument. **Voice:** clipped, businesslike, indignant when interrupted.

**Father Johan Merriksonn** — Human, m, ~55. Priest of Pelor. Kindly face, wispy white hair. Wants peace, wants his brother back. **Insight +5.** **Voice:** warm, slow, measured.

**Corel** — Human, m, ~50. Master shepherd. Quiet, respected. Lean, weathered. Wool tuft on vest. **Survival +4, WIS 14.** **Voice:** speaks rarely, the room listens when he does.

**Leanor Slatebeard** — Dwarf, f, ~110. Innkeeper at the Shepherd's Crook. Knows every secret in the village. Quietly powerful. **Voice:** cheerful, knowing, gossipy.

**Willen Featherock** — Halfling, m. Broken shepherd. Bandaged, sweat-soaked, jumpy. Will tell the truth if believed. **Voice:** trembling, hesitant, breaks into tears.

**Alexi Merriksonn** — *(Dead before the adventure starts)* Human, m. Welton's wild-magic sorcerer. Eccentric, passionate, the village's protector. Killed himself with a Wild Magic Surge while trying to drive off a wolf pack. His intelligence and elemental powers now live in **Flame and Bolt**.

**Flame** — Alpha female. Chaotic neutral. Fire breath. Wants to terrorize humans out of the region. Eyes burn orange.

**Bolt** — Alpha male. Lawful neutral. Lightning breath. Wants to retreat and live in peace. Speaks softly because his voice rumbles like thunder.

## TUNING NOTES

- **Party of 5 at level 3:** Add 2 wolves to Scene 1, give Flame 60 HP, give Bolt 60 HP.
- **Party of 3 at level 2:** Remove 2 wolves from Scene 1, drop Flame and Bolt to 35 HP each, cut Scene 4.
- **Lethal mode:** Flame uses Fire Breath the moment any PC is at half HP regardless of allies caught. Bolt uses Lightning Breath at max range to snipe casters.
- **Soft mode:** Flame and Bolt save breath weapons for round 3+. Wolves break and run at 1/3 HP individually.

## FAILURE / CHAOS BRANCHES

- **Party kills Bolt, lets Flame live:** Flame consolidates the pack, starts deliberate murder raids on Welton. Sequel hook: the village burns inside a month.
- **Party negotiates, then betrays:** Bolt's pack hunts them across the region. Pack tactics + 2 elemental alphas. They never travel safely through that province again.
- **Party tells the whole village the truth:** Welton splits. Half want peace, half want extermination. A second sorcerer arrives within a year, and the wolves either become allies of the village or its conquerors.
- **A PC dies to Flame's Fire Breath:** Bolt offers, in genuine remorse, to negotiate a body return and concessions. He won't undo the death but he'll make it mean something.
