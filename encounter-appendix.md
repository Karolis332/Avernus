### ROLL 7-8: THE TOLL COLLECTORS (Replaces generic hell hound pack)

**CR Budget:** 13 (Hard for 3 level-11 PCs) | **XP:** 10,900

**Concept:** A horned devil tax collector with hell hound enforcers running a tollgate on a narrow canyon pass. The devil doesn't want to fight — he wants payment. But his definition of "payment" is flexible and sinister.

#### Encounter Composition

| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Horned Devil ("Taxmaster Vorath") | 1 | 11 | 178 | 18 | 7,200 |
| Hell Hound | 4 | 3 | 45 | 15 | 2,800 |

**Total XP: 10,000 (adjusted 15,000 for 5 creatures)**

#### Terrain
Canyon pass, 30 ft wide, 80 ft long. Walls are 40 ft high (DC 18 Athletics to climb). A crude iron gate blocks the midpoint. Hell hounds patrol both ends — the party is boxed.

#### Vorath's Toll (Social First)
Vorath is a bureaucrat, not a berserker. He extends a clawed hand and speaks in Infernal:

> "Toll. Two soul coins per head, or one memory of value. Zariel's road, Zariel's rules. No exceptions. No credit."

**DC 16 Persuasion:** Reduce to 1 soul coin per head.
**DC 18 Deception:** Convince him you're working for Zariel (he'll radio ahead — creates problems later).
**DC 14 Intimidation:** He laughs. +2 to his initiative if combat starts. He respects strength.

**If they refuse AND won't negotiate:** Vorath sighs. "Pity." Combat begins.

#### Tactics (If Combat)
- **Round 1:** Vorath uses Hurl Flame (+7, 4d6 fire, 150 ft) targeting Aurora while hell hounds rush Drenwal (pack tactics, fire breath on cluster if 2+ PCs adjacent).
- **Round 2+:** Vorath flies (60 ft) above melee range, continues Hurl Flame. Uses Fork (+10, 2d8+6) only if someone reaches him. Hell hounds grapple Asimov to deny Sneak Attack flanking.
- **Infernal Wound (Tail):** Vorath saves this for Drenwal — bleed 3d6/turn forces the Cleric to heal himself instead of others. DC 12 Medicine or magical healing to stop.
- **Key puzzle:** Vorath FLIES. Asimov can't reach him easily. Aurora's Eldritch Blast is the primary damage source. If they don't ground him (grapple from above, dispel flight, Moonbow Banishment), this fight drags.

#### Drops
| Item | Source |
|------|--------|
| 3 soul coins | Vorath's toll lockbox |
| Infernal Toll Ledger | Lists recent travelers, including "3 Bhaalspawn" traveling south 2 days ago — Dumal intel |
| Hellfire Fork (+1 pike, 2d8+1 piercing, 1/day Hurl Flame as action) | Vorath's weapon. Not cursed. |

### ROLL 15-16: INFERNAL BOUNTY HUNTERS — "The Chain of Asmodeus"

**CR Budget:** 18 (Deadly) | **XP:** 16,600

**Concept:** A professional devil bounty hunting squad. They have a WARRANT — signed by an ice devil general — for "the mortals who disrupted the Moonkite containment facility." They know the party's names. They've studied the party's tactics.

#### Encounter Composition

| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Bone Devil (Commander "Vexiris") | 1 | 9 | 142 | 19 | 5,000 |
| Chain Devil ("The Binder") | 1 | 8 | 85 | 16 | 3,900 |
| Barbed Devil | 2 | 5 | 110 | 15 | 3,600 |

**Total XP: 12,500 (adjusted 18,750)**

#### Terrain
Narrow ravine. Chain devil has pre-set 4 animated chains across the ravine (AC 20, 20 HP each). The chains create a web — any creature moving through must DC 14 Dex or be grappled + restrained (2d6 piercing/round). The terrain is the chain devil's trap.

#### Tactics — They Know You

This squad has studied the party. Each devil has a specific target:

**Vexiris (Bone Devil) — targets Drenwal:**
- Opens combat by flying to Drenwal and using Sting (+8, 2d8+4 + 5d6 poison). The poison forces DC 14 Con — if Drenwal fails, he's poisoned (disadvantage on attacks and ability checks). This wrecks his concentration on Spirit Guardians AND reduces his healing output.
- Every turn: Sting Drenwal. The goal is to keep the healer poisoned.

**The Binder (Chain Devil) — targets Asimov:**
- Animated Chains are pre-set in the ravine. When Asimov tries to flank, a chain grapples and restrains him (escape DC 14). Restrained = no Sneak Attack advantage from flanking. The Binder uses its action to Chain attack restrained targets (+8, 2d6+4, auto-grapple).
- The Binder stays at range, letting the chains do the work. If Asimov breaks free, another chain grabs him.

**Barbed Devils — target Aurora:**
- Both Hurl Flame every round (+5, 3d6 fire, 150 ft). Two hits per round = two concentration checks if Aurora has a spell up. They're positioned on ledges (30 ft up) — melee can't reach them without climbing.
- Barbed Hide punishes anyone who grapples them (1d10 piercing/round).

**The Puzzle:** The party must break the pattern. If they fight their assigned opponents, they lose — the squad is designed to counter them. The KEY is target-swapping:
- Asimov should assassinate the barbed devils (low AC, climbable ledges, Sneak Attack from stealth)
- Aurora should Eldritch Blast the chain devil (low HP 85, force damage ignores resistances)
- Drenwal should use Radiance of the Dawn (AoE doesn't require attack rolls, hits everything in 30 ft) then focus-fire Vexiris with Spiritual Weapon

**Negotiation Option:** Vexiris will accept a bribe. **10 soul coins** or the Shield of the Hidden Lord (Gargauth). He'll claim the bounty is paid and file paperwork saying the party "resisted and escaped." DC 18 Persuasion to negotiate down to 5 soul coins + information about where Zariel's forces are weakest.

#### Drops
| Item | Source |
|------|--------|
| 3 soul coins | Bounty fund |
| Infernal Warrant | Names the party, details their crimes. Can be used as blackmail leverage with other devils, or burned to prevent further bounties (DC 16 Arcana to dispel the tracking magic on it) |
| Binding Chains (wondrous, rare) | 50 ft hellforged chain. Action: throw at creature within 15 ft, DC 16 Str or restrained. Escape DC 16 Str. Fiends restrained by this chain can't teleport. 1 use per long rest. |
| Venom Gland | 5 doses of bone devil venom (coat weapon, +2d6 poison, DC 14 Con or poisoned 1 hour) |

### ROLL 19-20: BLOOD WAR SPILLOVER — "The Butcher's Crossing"

**CR Budget:** 16 (Deadly) | **XP:** 14,100

**Concept:** Demons and devils are mid-battle when the party arrives. Both sides are weakened. The party can intervene, hide, or wait and loot the aftermath. But whoever wins will turn on them.

#### Encounter Composition — TWO SIDES

**Devil Side:**
| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Ice Devil (wounded) | 1 | 14 | 120/180 | 18 | 11,500 |
| Merregon | 3 | 4 | 30/45 | 18 | 3,300 |

**Demon Side:**
| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Vrock (wounded) | 2 | 6 | 70/104 | 15 | 4,600 |
| Dretch | 6 | 1/4 | 18 | 11 | 300 |

All creatures start at 2/3 HP (wounded from fighting each other).

**XP award:** The party gets XP only for creatures THEY defeat, not ones killed by the other side.

#### Terrain
Crossroads of two Avernus highways. Wrecked war machines burn. A 20-ft crater in the center (from an exploded soul coin engine) is difficult terrain and lightly obscured by smoke. Soul coins (2d4) are scattered in the crater.

#### What Happens If The Party...

**Watches and waits (DC 15 Stealth to stay hidden):**
- The ice devil wins in 3 rounds (demons are losing). It finishes wounded, ~60 HP remaining.
- It spots the party (passive Perception 12) and demands their soul coins. If refused, it attacks — but at 60 HP, it's a manageable fight.
- The dretches scatter. 2d4 soul coins are available in the aftermath.

**Helps the devils:**
- The ice devil is grateful but cold. It gives them a "Seal of Passage" — token that lets them pass ONE ice devil checkpoint without combat. Then it leaves.
- The dretches flee. Looting the demon corpses yields Vrock Feathers (alchemical component, worth 2 soul coins each to the right buyer).

**Helps the demons:**
- The vrocks are chaotic and ungrateful. After the devils die, DC 18 Persuasion to stop the vrocks from attacking the party too. On failure, the weakened vrocks attack (but at 40 HP each, they're manageable).
- Looting the devil corpses yields the ice devil's Frozen Signet Ring (1/day, cast Sleet Storm, DC 17, no concentration, lasts 1 minute).

**Attacks everyone:**
- Full brawl. Both sides turn on the party first (common enemy unites them for 1 round, then they resume fighting each other). Total chaos. High XP but high risk.

#### Puzzle
The puzzle is DIPLOMACY and TIMING. Fighting both sides is suicide. Picking a side has lasting consequences — devils remember allies, demons remember enemies. The ice devil recognizes the party if they've already been flagged by Zariel's forces (Rep 3+).

#### Drops (varies by outcome)
| Item | Source | Condition |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 2d4 soul coins | Scattered in crater | Always available |
| Seal of Passage (token) | Ice devil | Help devils win |
| Frozen Signet Ring (Sleet Storm 1/day) | Ice devil corpse | Kill the ice devil |
| Vrock Feathers (2, worth 2 SC each) | Vrock corpses | Kill vrocks |
| Demonic Ichor (3 doses, poison weapon 1d8 poison + DC 12 Con or stunned 1 round) | Vrock corpses | Kill vrocks |

### ROLL 21-22: ZARIEL'S ICE DEVIL PATROL — "The Frost Commission"

**CR Budget:** 20 (Deadly+) | **XP:** 18,200

**Concept:** This is not a random patrol. This is a HUNTING PARTY sent specifically after the celestial pulse from the Moonkite liberation. They have orders: capture alive if possible, kill if necessary, retrieve any celestial artifacts.

#### Encounter Composition

| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Ice Devil ("Commissioner Haelthos") | 1 | 14 | 180 | 18 | 11,500 |
| Barbed Devil (enforcers) | 2 | 5 | 110 | 15 | 3,600 |
| Spined Devil (scouts) | 4 | 2 | 22 | 13 | 1,800 |

**Total XP: 16,900 (adjusted 28,000 for 7 creatures)**

#### Terrain
Open wasteland with a frozen patch — 40-ft radius of supernaturally cold ground (Haelthos's aura). Difficult terrain for non-devils. Any creature that starts its turn on the ice: DC 14 Con or speed halved until end of turn. Three 15-ft-tall ice pillars (Haelthos's Wall of Ice, pre-cast) block sightlines and create chokepoints.

#### Haelthos — Commissioner of the Frost Commission

Haelthos is SMART. Ice devils are the strategists of the Nine Hells. He doesn't charge in — he controls the battlefield.

**Round 0 (before combat):** Haelthos has already cast Wall of Ice to create the three pillars. The battlefield is shaped before the party arrives. Spined devils are flying 60 ft up, circling.

**Opening move:** Haelthos calls out in Common:

> "By the authority of the Lord of the First, you are ordered to surrender all celestial artifacts and submit to interrogation. Compliance will be rewarded. Resistance will be... processed."

**DC 20 Persuasion/Deception** to talk out of this. Haelthos is not corruptible — he's a true believer in the hierarchy. But he can be TRICKED: if the party claims the Moonkite is already dead and its power was absorbed by a specific devil (name a rival of Zariel's), DC 18 Deception with a plausible lie, Haelthos will leave to verify.

#### Tactics

**Haelthos (Ice Devil):**
- **Wall of Ice** is the fight-winner. He casts it to split the party — wall between Drenwal and the other two PCs. The wall deals 10d6 cold (DC 17 Dex half) to creatures in its space. Each 10-ft section: AC 5, 30 HP, vulnerable to fire, immune to cold/acid/necrotic/poison/psychic.
- **Multiattack:** Bite +10 (2d6+5 + 3d6 cold), Claw +10 (2d4+5 + 3d6 cold), Tail +10 (2d6+5 + 3d6 cold). Average ~55 damage per round.
- **Key tactic:** Tail attack has 10-ft reach. Haelthos stands behind his Wall of Ice sections and tails PCs over the wall without exposing himself to melee.

**Barbed Devils (enforcers):**
- Position on opposite sides of the ice pillars. Hurl Flame at whoever Haelthos isn't fighting. Force concentration checks on any buff spells.
- If a PC is isolated by Wall of Ice, both barbed devils focus that PC.

**Spined Devils (scouts):**
- Flyby harassment. Tail spines (+4, 1d4+2 + 1d6 fire) from 100 ft up. They never land. They're annoying, not dangerous — but they force Asimov to waste actions on them or ignore the chip damage.
- **If the party has the Lady Vengeance nearby:** Two spined devils break off to sabotage the ship (set fire to soul coin reserves, DC 15 Perception to notice).

#### The Puzzle

Wall of Ice is the fight. If the party can't get past/through/around the walls, Haelthos picks them off one at a time from safety. Solutions:
- **Fire damage** destroys wall sections fast (vulnerability = double damage)
- **Eldritch Blast** (force damage) can target the wall
- **Misty Escape** (Aurora's reaction teleport) gets past walls
- **Moonbow Banishment** sends Haelthos to the Feywild for 1 minute (the walls stay, but the brains of the operation is gone — the barbed devils will fight on autopilot)

**Reinforcements:** If the fight goes past 6 rounds, Haelthos sends a spined devil for reinforcements. 1d4 hours later, another ice devil arrives. The party should feel pressure to win FAST or flee.

**Retreat option:** Haelthos will NOT chase if the party flees at full speed. He's methodical — he'll track them and set up another ambush in 12-24 hours. Running doesn't solve the problem, it delays it.

#### Drops
| Item | Source |
|------|--------|
| 4 soul coins | Haelthos's war chest |
| Frost Commission Warrant | Zariel's orders. Details the Moonkite liberation. Lists the party. Can be destroyed (DC 16 Arcana) to end the active warrant — but Zariel will issue a new one in 1d4 days. |
| Ice Devil's Heart (wondrous, very rare) | Cold resistance. 1/LR: cast Cone of Cold (DC 17, 8d8 cold). Attunement required. While attuned, your skin develops a faint blue tinge — fiends recognize you as a "devil-killer" (advantage on Intimidation vs fiends, disadvantage on Persuasion vs fiends). |
| Infernal Tack Fragment | Part of a nightmare's saddle. If the party collects 3 fragments total, they can summon a Nightmare mount (future quest hook). |

### CRYPT OF THE HELLRIDERS (Olanthius)

**Travel:** 8-12 hours | **Navigation DC:** 12 (Near) | **Map:** Underground crypt, 3 levels

#### Level 1: The Fallen Hall
| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Wight (Corrupted Hellrider) | 4 | 3 | 45 | 14 | 2,800 |
| Specter | 6 | 1 | 22 | 12 | 1,200 |

**CR Budget: 14 | XP: 4,000 (adjusted 12,000)**

**Terrain:** Long hall lined with stone sarcophagi. Dim light from infernal braziers. Specters phase through walls — melee can't pin them without Readied actions.

**Tactics:** Wights use Life Drain (+4, 1d6+2 necrotic, DC 13 Con or max HP reduced). They focus Drenwal — if a divine caster falls, they drain faster. Specters harass Aurora from inside walls (Life Drain +4, 3d6 necrotic, DC 10 Con or max HP reduced). They never fully emerge — half cover from walls.

**Puzzle:** The specters can't be reached in the walls. Radiance of the Dawn ignores cover (it's an emanation, not an attack). Drenwal is the key — his Channel Divinity clears the specters in one use. But that burns a resource he'll need later.

**Drops:** 2 soul coins in sarcophagi, Hellrider's Medal (advantage vs fear 24h, 1 use), Scroll of Revivify.

#### Level 2: The Bone Scriptorium
| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Deathlock Mastermind | 2 | 6 | 110 | 13 | 4,600 |
| Zombie (shieldbearers) | 8 | 1/4 | 22 | 8 | 400 |

**CR Budget: 14 | XP: 5,000 (adjusted 15,000)**

**Terrain:** Library of bone-carved tablets. Bookshelves provide cover. A summoning circle in the center glows — the deathlocks use it to raise more zombies (1d4 per round if not disrupted, DC 16 Arcana to break).

**Tactics:** Deathlocks cast from cover: Blight (4th level, 8d8 necrotic), Counterspell (3rd level — they WILL counter Drenwal's heals), Darkness (centered on themselves, they have Devil's Sight). Zombies are shieldbearers — they use Undead Fortitude AND stand in front of the deathlocks.

**Puzzle:** The summoning circle creates infinite zombies. The deathlocks have Counterspell. If the party tries to grind through zombies while the deathlocks keep summoning, they lose to attrition. Solutions:
- Break the summoning circle FIRST (DC 16 Arcana, full action, provokes from adjacent zombies)
- Kill the deathlocks directly (they're fragile at 110 HP, AC 13 — but they hide in Darkness)
- Asimov's Psychic Blades ignore Darkness (psychic damage, he can use Psychic Whispers to coordinate in the dark)

**Drops:** 2 soul coins, Scroll of Remove Curse, Olanthius's Journal Fragment (reveals he regrets serving Zariel, hints at where to find him on Level 3).

#### Level 3: Olanthius's Chamber (Social/Combat)

Olanthius is a **Death Knight** (CR 17, AC 20, HP 180). He is NOT hostile unless the party attacks first or desecrates the crypt. He sits on a throne of blackened Hellrider armor, his greatsword across his knees.

> "More mortals. The crypt draws them like moths. Speak, or leave. I have waited centuries — a few more minutes changes nothing."

**Olanthius's Information (free, if asked respectfully):**
- Zariel's history — she was an angel who fell trying to fight the Blood War alone
- The Bleeding Citadel's purpose — it contains Zariel's Sword, the weapon that can redeem or destroy her
- Bel's location and how to approach him (Bel was the previous Lord of Avernus, deposed by Zariel)

**Olanthius's Task:** He wants the party to free Kostchtchie from the Maw. The demon lord's rage will distract Zariel's forces, creating an opening for the party to reach the Arches of Ulloch. He won't fight alongside the party, but he'll give them his Hellrider Shield (+2 shield, advantage on saves vs fiend magic) if they agree.

**If combat:** Olanthius is a CR 17 Death Knight. He has Hellfire Orb (10d6 fire + 10d6 necrotic in 20-ft sphere, DC 18 Dex). He casts Destructive Wave (5d6 thunder + 5d6 necrotic, 30-ft radius) on round 1. This fight is NEARLY UNWINNABLE for 3 level-11 PCs. Do not let them fight him unless they've been warned.

**Drops (social resolution):** Hellrider Shield (+2 shield, advantage on saves vs fiend spells/abilities), directions to Bel's Forge, lore about the Arches of Ulloch.

### BEL'S FORGE

**Travel:** Varies | **Navigation DC:** 15 (Mid) | **Map:** Volcanic forge complex

#### The Test of Worthiness — 4 Fire Giants
| Creature | Count | CR | HP | AC | XP |
|----------|-------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Fire Giant | 4 | 9 | 162 | 18 | 20,000 |

**CR Budget: 20+ (Deadly) | XP: 20,000**

**This fight is DESIGNED to be near-impossible head-on.** Bel watches from above. He wants to see if the party fights SMART, not just hard.

**Terrain:** Forge floor with lava channels (2d10 fire per round if you fall in, DC 15 Dex to avoid falling when pushed). Anvils provide half cover. Giant-forged chains hang from the ceiling (climbable, DC 14 Athletics). A massive bellows system can be activated (DC 16 Athletics or Investigation) — blasts a 30-ft cone of superheated air (6d6 fire, DC 17 Dex half) once per round.

**Tactics:** Fire giants throw rocks (+11, 4d10+7 bludgeoning, 60/240 ft) for rounds 1-2 while PCs approach. In melee: Greatsword +11, 6d6+7 slashing. They fight in pairs — two hold the line, two throw rocks. They kick prone PCs into lava channels.

**Puzzle:** 4 fire giants is WAY too much for 3 level-11 PCs in a straight fight. The forge environment is the answer:
- **Lava channels:** Push/pull giants into them (Repelling Blast, Thunderwave). Giants take 2d10 fire per round and must use their action to climb out.
- **Bellows:** 6d6 fire cone, reusable. Position it to hit multiple giants.
- **Chains:** Fighting from the chains (15 ft up) forces giants to throw rocks (less damage) or spend actions pulling PCs down.
- **Bel's approval:** If the party uses the forge creatively (at least 2 environmental kills or tricks), Bel stops the fight early. "Enough. You think. I can use thinkers."

**If the party is losing:** Bel calls it off at 2 downed PCs. "You're not ready. Come back when you are." No shame — this is a test, not an execution.

**Drops (from Bel, not the giants):** Bel's information (directions to Arches of Ulloch, weakness of the sphinx guardian), Bel's Signet (advantage on Persuasion with devils, identifies you as Bel's agent — dangerous if Zariel finds out).

#### Bel's Task: Wrecked Flying Fortress
